## 1. US Imposes 50% Tariffs on Some Canadian Goods 

The US government has put a **50% tariff** on certain goods from Canada. This means these products will cost more when sold in the US. The reason given is that Canada is **discriminating** against American products. Tariffs are taxes on imports, and they can affect trade between countries. This decision might make Canadian goods more expensive and could cause tension between the two neighbors. Businesses and consumers in both countries may feel the impact. We will need to wait and see if Canada responds or if the two governments find a way to solve this problem.

> - **tariff** — 관세, 수입세  
>   _The government imposed a **tariff** on imported cars._  
> - **discrimination** — 차별

> - **put a tariff on** — ~에 관세를 부과하다  
>   _The country decided to **put a tariff on** steel imports._  
> - **trade tension** — 무역 긴장

## 2. The Need for Better Immigration Policies in America 

Many experts say that the current US immigration system is full of **cruelty** and **recklessness**. This means it is unfair and dangerous for many immigrants. The Center for American Progress calls for new immigration policies that are stronger and kinder. They argue that better policies will help America grow and be safer. Immigration affects many parts of life, including the economy and communities. Good policies can protect human rights and support the country’s future. People are hoping that lawmakers will listen and make important changes soon.

## 3. Fewer Jobs Were Added in June in the US 

In June, many US employers decided to **pull back on hiring**. This means they hired fewer workers than before. Reports show that the job market is not as strong as people expected. Some experts say this could be a sign that the economy is slowing down. When fewer jobs are created, it can be harder for people to find work. This news is important because jobs affect many parts of life, like income and spending. People will watch the next reports to see if this trend continues.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **pull back on hiring** — 채용을 줄이다  
>   _The company had to **pull back on hiring** because of budget cuts._  
> - **job market** — 노동 시장, 취업 시장  
>   _The **job market** is very competitive right now._  
> - **weaker-than-expected** — 예상보다 약한  
>   _Sales were **weaker-than-expected** last quarter._  
> - **sign of** — ~의 신호  
>   _This slow growth is a **sign of** economic problems._

## 4. Why Silicon Valley Keeps Watching China Closely 

Silicon Valley, the US technology center, is **looking over its shoulder** at China. This means tech companies are worried about China’s actions and how it affects their business. The relationship between the US and China is complex, with competition and concerns about technology sharing. Many companies are careful about working with China because of political risks. This situation makes Silicon Valley feel nervous and uncertain about the future. Experts say this will affect how technology grows and where companies decide to invest.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **look over its shoulder** — 뒤를 조심스럽게 살피다  
>   _The company is **looking over its shoulder** because of new competitors._  
> - **tech company** — 기술 회사  
>   _Many **tech companies** are developing new software._  
> - **political risk** — 정치적 위험  
>   _Investors avoid areas with high **political risk**._  
> - **affect growth** — 성장을 영향을 미치다  
>   _New regulations can **affect growth** in the industry._
