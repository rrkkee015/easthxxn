## 1. U.S. Launches Airstrikes Against Iran for Second Night 

The United States has carried out airstrikes against Iran for the second night in a row. These attacks come after rising tensions between the two countries. The strikes targeted areas believed to be linked to Iranian-backed groups. This is an important development because it shows the U.S. is serious about responding to threats in the region. Experts say this could lead to more conflict or efforts to find a peaceful solution. The situation is still **unfolding**, and many are watching closely to see what happens next. This story affects international relations and U.S. security policies.

A recent hearing in the U.S. House of Representatives focused on the abuse of immigration rules. Lawmakers discussed how some people use the system unfairly. The hearing aims to find ways to protect the policy and help real applicants. This is important because immigration affects many families and the economy. The debate shows how immigration is a complicated issue with many opinions. Changes to the policy could come soon, affecting who can stay in the country and how. Many people are waiting to see the results of this hearing.

This news is important because jobs affect people’s daily lives and the country’s economy. Many hope that job growth will continue and help improve living standards. The government may try new policies to support workers.

This shows how powerful the tech sector is in politics today. These election results could affect laws on privacy, business, and innovation. The tech industry’s influence means it can help shape California’s future economy. Voters are paying more attention to technology’s role in their daily lives. This news is a sign of how technology and politics are connected.
