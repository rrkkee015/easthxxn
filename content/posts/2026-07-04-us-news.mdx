## 1. Trump’s Speech at Mount Rushmore Sparks Attention 

Former President Donald Trump is set to speak at Mount Rushmore during the America 250 celebrations. This monument is famous and cannot be changed, which makes this event very special. The speech comes as the country faces very hot weather and mixed feelings about the celebration. Many people are interested because Mount Rushmore is a symbol of American history, but some also find it controversial. The event shows how leaders use important places to send messages. People will watch closely to see what Trump says and how it affects public opinion. This moment reminds us how history and politics often connect during big national events.

## 3. U.S. Job Growth Slows Down in June 

In June, the United States added only 57,000 new jobs, a big drop from previous months. This shows that job creation has **cooled** and the economy is growing more slowly. The unemployment rate stayed at 4.2%, which means many people still want work. Experts say this change can affect many industries and workers. Some think the slower growth might continue, while others hope it is just a short pause. The job market is important because it tells us how healthy the economy is. People will watch future reports to see if hiring picks up again.
