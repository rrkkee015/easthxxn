## 1. Election Night: Early Returns Show California Voters in Action  
  

The polls have just closed in California, and early election results are coming in. People across the state **turned out** to vote for various candidates, including the mayor of Los Angeles who faces tough competition from a TV star and a former ally. This election is important because it will decide local leaders who affect daily life and policies. Watching early returns helps us understand how people **cast their ballots** and what the future might look like politically. It also shows which issues matter most to voters right now. Results will be clearer as more votes are counted, so the situation could still change. This election could **set the tone** for other states and upcoming national elections.

## 2. Governor Moore Discusses Immigration's Economic Impact  
  

Governor Wes Moore recently held a roundtable discussion about how federal immigration policies affect the economy. Leaders and experts talked about the ways immigration can help businesses grow and create jobs. The governor wants to find a balance between keeping communities safe and welcoming new workers. This discussion is important because immigration rules can change how many people can come to work or live in the US. Understanding these effects can help create better laws that support both the economy and families. The governor plans to use this meeting to suggest new ideas to federal officials. It shows how local leaders try to **stay ahead of** big challenges.

## 3. US Job Openings Reach High Levels Despite Challenges  
  

In April, the US had 7.6 million job openings. This number went up even though there are worries about the economy because of conflicts like the Iran war. The job market is still strong, but some reports say hiring is slower than expected. Many companies want to find workers, but they are careful because the future is uncertain. This trend shows that businesses are **open for business**, but they might wait before hiring more people. It is important because jobs help the economy grow and give people income. Experts will watch the data closely to see how the job market changes in the coming months.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **job openings** — 일자리 공석  
>   _There are many **job openings** in the tech industry right now._  
> - **open for business** — 영업 중, 준비 완료  
>   _The restaurant is **open for business** after remodeling._  
> - **uncertain future** — 불확실한 미래  
>   _Many workers worry about the **uncertain future** of their jobs._

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Role in Ukraine’s Defense with AI Technology  
  

Ukraine’s president, Zelenskyy, is asking Silicon Valley defense startups to bring their artificial intelligence (AI) technology to help in the war. He says Ukraine will provide battle experience while tech companies bring smart machines and software. This partnership could change how wars are fought in the future. It shows how technology and private companies are becoming more important in defense, not just governments. AI can help find dangers faster and make better decisions on the battlefield. This idea is exciting but also raises questions about the rules of war and the role of technology. Silicon Valley’s involvement could **push the boundaries** of how AI is used in real life.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **bring your AI** — 너의 인공지능을 가져오다 (여기서는 기술을 제공하다)  
>   _The company asked engineers to **bring their AI** ideas to the project._  
> - **push the boundaries** — 한계를 넘다, 한계를 넓히다  
>   _This new method **pushes the boundaries** of medical science._  
> - **battle experience** — 전투 경험  
>   _Veterans share their **battle experience** to train new soldiers._
