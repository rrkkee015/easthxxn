## 1. US Economy Adds 259,000 Jobs in June, Showing Steady Growth  


The US economy added 259,000 jobs in June, according to a recent report. This number shows that the job market is **turning a corner** after some slow months. More people are finding work, and the unemployment rate is holding steady. Experts say this steady job growth is a good sign for the economy. However, some worry about inflation and other problems that could affect future hiring. The government will keep watching the numbers closely to see if this **trend continues**. Workers in many industries hope this means better opportunities soon.

## 2. Tech Industry Faces Challenges with Trump’s Changing AI Policies  


The tech world is **grappling with** new changes in AI policies from former President Trump. These changes come after Trump made sudden **about-faces** on important AI rules. Many tech companies feel confused and worried about what will happen next. AI, or artificial intelligence, is a key part of many tech products today. The industry wants clear rules so it can continue to grow safely. Experts say that without good guidance, innovation could slow down. The government and tech leaders will likely talk more to find a solution.

## 3. New Immigration Rules Allow Local Police to Help Enforce Federal Laws  


The US government has updated immigration rules, letting some local police help with federal immigration enforcement. This is part of Section 287(g) of the Immigration and Nationality Act. The change means local officers can work with federal agents to find and hold people who are in the country illegally. Supporters say this will keep communities safer. But critics worry it could lead to unfair treatment of immigrants. This update shows how immigration remains a **key issue** in America. More discussions are expected about how to balance safety and fairness.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **enforce federal laws** — 연방 법률을 집행하다  
>   _Local police will **enforce federal laws** with federal agents._  
> - **key issue** — 중요한 문제  
>   _Immigration is a **key issue** in many countries._  
> - **hold people** — 사람들을 구금하다  
>   _Officers can **hold people** suspected of breaking the law._

## 4. US Declines to Extend North American Trade Deal, Starting Clock to End It  


The US recently decided not to extend the North American trade deal with Canada and Mexico. This decision starts a countdown to possibly end the agreement. At the same time, the US wants to make some changes to the deal. The trade agreement is very important for business and jobs in all three countries. Ending it could cause problems for companies that trade goods across borders every day. Leaders will meet soon to try to fix the issues before the deal ends. Many hope they can agree on updates and keep the trade deal alive.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **trade deal** — 무역 협정  
>   _The countries signed a new **trade deal** last year._  
> - **starting the clock** — 시간을 재기 시작하다, 시한이 시작되다  
>   _By refusing to extend, the US is **starting the clock** on ending the deal._  
> - **make changes** — 변경하다  
>   _They want to **make changes** to improve the agreement._
