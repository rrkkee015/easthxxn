## 1. New Rule Requires Most Green-Card Applicants to Apply from Outside the U.S.  


The U.S. government has introduced a new rule about green-card applications. Now, most people who want to get a green card must apply from outside the United States. This means they cannot apply for the green card while staying inside the country. The rule is part of the Trump administration’s efforts to change immigration policies. It affects many immigrants who are already living in the U.S. but want to become permanent residents. The government says this rule will help control immigration better. However, immigration advocates worry it will make the process harder and longer for many people. This change may cause delays and more travel for applicants in the future.

## 2. U.S. Job Market Shows Strength Despite Layoffs  


Even though some companies have announced layoffs, the overall U.S. job market remains strong. Recent reports show that the number of people filing for unemployment benefits is low. This means many people are still keeping their jobs or finding new ones quickly. Experts say layoffs do not always show the real picture of the job market. Some industries are growing, and new jobs are being created. The economy still has some uncertainty, but for now, workers are in a better position than expected. This news is important for people looking for jobs or concerned about their employment future.

## 3. Silicon Valley Pressures Block Trump’s AI Order  


The Trump administration planned to issue an order about artificial intelligence (AI), but it has been blocked. Many important tech companies in Silicon Valley pushed back against the order. They worried the new rules would slow down innovation and technology development. Silicon Valley is known as a global center for technology and startups. The pressure from these companies shows their power in shaping government decisions. AI is a fast-growing field, and how it is regulated will affect many industries. This story highlights the ongoing debate about balancing technology growth and safety.

## 4. What You Need to Know About the New USCIS Adjustment of Status Policy  


The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) has changed its policy about adjustment of status. Adjustment of status lets some immigrants apply to become permanent residents while staying in the U.S. The new policy requires most applicants to start their green card process from outside the country. This is a big change from before. The government says it will help make the immigration process clearer and more secure. But many immigrants and lawyers are concerned it will cause problems. This update affects many families and workers who want to live legally in the U.S.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **adjustment of status** — 신분 조정 (미국 내에서 영주권 신청 절차)  
>   _Many immigrants use **adjustment of status** to become permanent residents._  
> - **applicant** — 지원자, 신청자  
>   _Every **applicant** must follow the new rules carefully._  
> - **immigration process** — 이민 절차  
>   _The **immigration process** can take several months or even years._  
> - **policy change** — 정책 변경  
>   _The **policy change** affects how people apply for green cards._
