## 1. Markets React to Trump’s Speech on Iran and Rising Oil Prices  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-02-1.png)  

Recently, former President Trump made a speech about potential conflict with Iran. This speech caused worry in financial markets. Oil prices **rose sharply** because investors fear a war could disrupt oil supplies. When oil prices go up, it can make many goods more expensive for people. The stock market also felt nervous because uncertainty often makes investors **pull back** from risky investments. Experts say this situation could affect the economy if tensions grow. People are watching closely to see if political leaders will calm the situation or if it will get worse.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **rise sharply** — 급격히 오르다  
>   _Oil prices **rose sharply** after the news._  
> - **pull back** — 철수하다, 물러나다  
>   _Investors tend to **pull back** during uncertain times._  
> - **financial markets** — 금융시장  
>   _**Financial markets** reacted quickly to the speech._  
> - **disrupt** — 방해하다, 중단시키다  
>   _A war could **disrupt** oil supplies._

## 2. Federal Policy Changes Impacting the American Dream  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-02-2.png)  

Recent shifts in U.S. immigration policies are changing how people experience the American Dream. Some new federal rules make it harder for immigrants to stay and work in the U.S. Experts warn these changes could **erode** hopes for many families. The American Dream is about having a chance for a better life, but policy shifts may **stand in the way** of that goal. These changes come from debates about citizenship, border security, and who can enter the country. Many people are concerned about what will happen next and how these policies will affect communities.

## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Strength Despite Concerns  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-02-3.png)  

In March, hiring in the private sector was strong, showing that companies are still adding workers. However, some experts worry about the future because other reports show fewer job openings and slower hiring. The job market is important because it affects how much money people earn and how confident they feel. When hiring slows, it can mean companies expect tougher times ahead. Still, for now, many businesses keep hiring to meet demand. Economists will watch more data to see if the job market stays healthy or if it will weaken soon.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **private sector** — 민간 부문  
>   _Most new jobs were in the **private sector**._  
> - **job openings** — 일자리 공석  
>   _The number of **job openings** has fallen._  
> - **hiring** — 채용, 고용  
>   _Companies are still **hiring** new workers._  
> - **job market** — 구직 시장  
>   _The **job market** affects many people's lives._

## 4. Silicon Valley and Washington Need to Collaborate on Tech Innovation  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-02-4.png)  

A recent article says Silicon Valley and the U.S. government must work **together** to build the future of technology. The author argues that tech companies and government agencies should **join forces** to solve big challenges. This cooperation could help the U.S. stay a leader in innovation and compete globally. There are many areas like artificial intelligence and cybersecurity where teamwork is important. If they keep working separately, they might **fall behind** other countries. Experts believe that building trust and sharing resources will be key to success.
