## 1. Rising Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz as Ship Attacks Increase  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-12-1.png)  

The Strait of Hormuz is a very important waterway for world oil supply. Recently, attacks on ships in this area have **escalated**, causing worry among many countries. Iran is believed to be involved in these attacks, increasing tensions between Iran and other nations. This conflict could affect global oil prices and shipping safety. Many countries are watching closely because the Strait is a key route for oil exports. If the situation gets worse, it may lead to bigger international problems. For now, countries are trying to **downplay** the risk but are also preparing for possible dangers.

## 2. Deep Political Divide on Immigration in the US  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-12-2.png)  

A new national survey shows a big **partisan chasm** in the United States about immigration. This means people from different political parties have very different opinions. Many Democrats support more open immigration policies, while many Republicans want stricter rules. This divide makes it hard to create immigration laws that everyone agrees on. Immigration is an important topic because it affects jobs, communities, and the economy. The survey helps us understand why debates about immigration are so heated. It also shows the challenge for leaders to find a solution that can bring people together.

## 3. Job Losses Rise and Unemployment Grows in February  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-12-3.png)  

In February, the US lost 92,000 jobs, and the unemployment rate went up to 4.4%. This is worrying because many people depend on jobs to support their families. The job market affects the whole economy, so these numbers can mean slower growth. Experts say that some industries, like restaurants, have not fully recovered from past problems. When jobs are lost, spending goes down, which can hurt businesses. The government and companies may need to work together to create more jobs. Watching the job market helps us understand how the economy is doing overall.

## 4. US Tech Firms and Government Clash Over AI Use in Warfare  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-12-4.png)  

In the United States, many tech companies are in conflict with the government about using artificial intelligence (AI) in war. Some tech firms do not want their AI technology to be used for military weapons. They worry about the risks and ethical problems. The government thinks AI can help protect the country and improve defense. This disagreement shows how new technology can cause big debates about safety and responsibility. The future of AI in warfare is uncertain, and many people are watching closely. Both sides want to find the best way to handle this powerful technology.
