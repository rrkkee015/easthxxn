## 1. Supreme Court Allows Access to Abortion Pill While Case Continues  


The U.S. Supreme Court recently made an important decision about the abortion pill. Even though there is a lawsuit challenging its use, the court said people can still get the pill while the case is going on. This means pharmacies can continue selling the pill for now. The abortion pill is a medicine that ends early pregnancy and is used by many women in the U.S. The court’s decision shows that access to this medicine is still protected for the moment. This case will continue in lower courts, so the final outcome might change in the future. Many people are watching closely because it affects women’s health rights across the country.

## 3. U.S. Job Market Remains Strong Despite Challenges  


The U.S. job market is showing good signs, with more jobs created than expected recently. This is good news for the economy because when more people have jobs, they spend money, and businesses grow. However, the market is still hard for some groups, especially recent college graduates. Many young people find it difficult to get their first good job. Experts say the job market is very competitive, and workers need strong skills to succeed. The government and companies are watching these trends closely to help improve job opportunities for everyone.

## 4. Indian Tech Workers in U.S. Face Challenges from Cultural Differences  


Many Indian tech workers in the U.S. say they face problems because of differences in work culture between the two countries. In India, some companies have a very strict hierarchy, meaning bosses have a lot of control and decisions go from the top down. This can feel **toxic** or unhealthy to workers used to a different style in America. The workers say this culture makes it hard to express ideas freely or feel comfortable at work. Understanding these cultural differences is important as many Indian professionals help grow the U.S. tech industry. Companies may need to change their management styles to support all workers better.
