## 1. House Passes DHS Funding Bill to End Most Shutdowns  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-05-01-1.png)  

The U.S. House of Representatives recently approved a bill to fund the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). This bill aims to **end the shutdown** that affected many parts of the DHS. A government shutdown happens when Congress does not agree on funding, which can stop many government services. The new funding will allow most DHS operations to continue without interruption. This is good news for workers and citizens who depend on DHS services like border security and immigration processing. However, some parts of the DHS may still face delays or limited budgets. The bill now moves to the Senate for approval, and if passed, the shutdown will officially be over.

## 2. Trump’s Immigration Restrictions Could Slow U.S. Population Growth  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-05-01-2.png)  

New rules under the Trump administration have made it harder for people to enter the U.S. legally. These **restrictions on legal immigration** may cause the U.S. population to grow more slowly in the future. Immigration has been an important part of America’s growth and economy for many years. Limiting immigration could affect labor markets, businesses, and communities that depend on new workers. Experts warn that fewer immigrants might lead to fewer workers, especially in important industries like healthcare and technology. The U.S. government is watching to see how these changes will affect the country over time.

## 3. Majority of US Job Seekers Want Formal AI Training  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-05-01-3.png)  

A new report shows that 83% of job seekers in the U.S. want **formal AI training**. Artificial intelligence (AI) is becoming very important in many jobs and industries. People want to learn more about AI to improve their skills and find better jobs. Training programs help workers understand how to use AI tools safely and effectively. Employers also want workers who know about AI because it can increase productivity. This demand may lead to more schools and companies offering AI courses in the future, making AI knowledge a key part of the job market.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s New Political Shift Shapes Tech Industry  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-05-01-4.png)  

Silicon Valley is known as the center of the tech world, but now a new group called the “Silicon Valley Right” is changing the **tech landscape**. This group has different political views than the traditional tech leaders. Their ideas are influencing company decisions, hiring, and the future of technology development. The change may affect how tech companies work with the government and handle issues like free speech and privacy. Experts say this shift could create new challenges but also open opportunities for innovation. The tech industry is watching closely to see how this will develop.
