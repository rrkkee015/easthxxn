## 1. U.S. Military Begins Blockade on Iranian Ports in Strait of Hormuz  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-14-1.png)

The U.S. military has started a **blockade** on Iranian ports located in the Strait of Hormuz. This area is very important because a lot of the world's oil passes through it. The blockade means that ships cannot enter or leave these ports easily. The U.S. says this action is to stop Iran from moving weapons or materials that could cause problems in the region. The situation is tense, and many countries are watching closely. This **blockade** could affect global oil prices and increase political **tensions** between the U.S. and Iran. Experts warn that the situation might get worse if the two sides do not find a way to talk and solve their problems peacefully.

## 2. Latest Immigration Policies Affect Foreign Doctors and Migrants  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-14-2.png)

Recent U.S. immigration policies are having a big impact on many people, including foreign doctors and migrants in transit countries. The U.S. is facing a shortage of doctors, but new rules make it harder for foreign medical professionals to work in the country. This can affect hospitals and patients who need care. At the same time, migration through other countries is changing because of U.S. policies. Some migrants are stuck in difficult situations while waiting for answers about their status. These immigration rules are important because they affect the health system and how people move between countries. Experts suggest that changes might be needed to help both the healthcare system and migrants.

> - **impact** — 영향, 영향력  (두 칸 띄우기!!)

> - **transit countries** — 경유국, 중간 국가  (두 칸 띄우기!!)

## 3. College Graduates Struggle to Find Entry-Level Jobs in AI-Changed Market  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-14-3.png)

Many recent college graduates feel **helpless** because they cannot find entry-level jobs. The job market is shrinking, and the rise of artificial intelligence (AI) changes how companies hire people. Some jobs that used to be available are now done by machines or AI programs. This situation makes it harder for young people to start their careers. Experts say that graduates need to learn new skills and adapt to this change. The problem is serious because work experience is important for future success. If the job market continues to shrink, more graduates might feel discouraged about their futures.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s AI Talent Moves Back to China  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-14-4.png)

Many top AI experts who once worked in Silicon Valley are now returning to China. China's government and companies are offering good money and support to bring their talent back home. This move is important because Silicon Valley has been the center of AI innovation for a long time. The return of these experts might change where new AI ideas and products are made. It also shows how competition between countries affects technology. Some worry that losing this talent could slow down AI progress in the U.S. But it also means China wants to become a leader in AI technology faster than before.
