## 1. ICE Told to Stop Most Vehicle Stops After Two Fatal Shootings in Maine and Texas  


Recently, ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) was told to **halt** most vehicle stops. This decision came after two people were **killed** during these stops in Maine and Texas. The shootings raised serious concerns about the safety of these actions. ICE says vehicle stops are important for immigration enforcement, but critics argue they put people at risk. Now, ICE will focus on other ways to enforce the law without stopping so many vehicles. This change shows how important it is to balance law enforcement with public safety. We will see if this approach reduces conflicts in the future.

## 2. House Passes Bill to Make Daylight Saving Time Permanent  


The US House of Representatives recently approved a plan to keep daylight saving time all year. This means we would not change our clocks twice a year anymore. Supporters say this change will help people have more daylight in the evening and avoid confusion. Some experts believe it could improve health by reducing sleep problems. However, there are still questions about how this will affect different parts of the country. If the Senate agrees, the US will stop the twice-yearly clock changes. This could be a big change in how Americans live daily.

## 3. US Job Growth Slows Despite Many Open Positions  


In June, the US job growth slowed down even though many jobs are open. Employers are hiring fewer workers than before. Experts say there is still a big **gap** between the number of job openings and new hires. Some companies find it hard to find the right workers. This slow hiring may affect the economy and people's plans for work. The White House says the labor market is still strong and expects it to get better soon. Watching these changes helps us understand how the job market is doing.

## 4. American Tech Companies Feel Pressure as China Advances in AI  


American technology companies are feeling nervous as China quickly improves in artificial intelligence (AI). For years, the US led in AI research and products, but now China is catching up fast. This increase in competition may change the future of tech industries worldwide. US companies are working hard to keep their position, but the new challenge is big. Some experts say this competition can lead to faster innovation. However, it also raises questions about global leadership in technology. Watching these changes will be important for the tech world.
