## 3. Millions Locked Out Despite Job Market Improvement  
  

The U.S. job market is getting better, but about two million workers are still locked out. This means they cannot find jobs even though there are many openings. Experts say there is a **hiring gap** because some workers do not have the skills companies want. Also, some businesses are slowing down their hiring even with openings available. This situation worries many people who want to work but face challenges. The government and companies may need to find new ways to train workers. Fixing this problem is important for the economy to grow stronger.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **locked out** — 제외되다, 배제되다  
>   _Many job seekers feel **locked out** of the current job market._  
> - **hiring gap** — 채용 격차  
>   _There is a big **hiring gap** between available jobs and qualified workers._

Experts say that the U.S. must keep investing in technology to stay ahead. This race between countries shows how technology affects global power.
