## 1. Lindsey Graham’s Sister Takes Over His Senate Seat 

The US Senate will now have Darline Graham Nordone serving the rest of Lindsey Graham’s term. Lindsey Graham is a well-known senator from South Carolina. He recently left his seat, and his sister was chosen to fill it. This is important because it keeps the seat in the family and maintains political stability for their state. Senators make big decisions about laws and policies that affect the whole country. Darline will have to get used to new responsibilities quickly. People are watching to see how she will handle her new role and if she will continue her brother’s work or bring new ideas.

> - **serve out a term** — 임기 동안 직무를 수행하다  
>   _She will **serve out the term** left by her brother._

>   _The appointment brings **political stability** to the state._

## 2. Banks Urged to Check Loans to Immigrants Without Work Permission 

The Trump administration has asked banks to be careful when lending money to immigrants who do not have work permission. This means banks should check more closely before giving loans to some immigrants. The government wants to reduce risks for banks but this can make it harder for many immigrants to get loans. Loans are important because they help people buy homes, start businesses, or pay for education. Some groups worry this will unfairly block immigrants from financial help. It is a big topic because it affects many families trying to build a better life in the US.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **scrutinize lending** — 대출을 자세히 조사하다  
>   _The bank must **scrutinize lending** to protect itself._  
> - **work authorization** — 취업 허가  
>   _Without **work authorization**, it is harder to get a loan._  
> - **reduce risks** — 위험을 줄이다  
>   _The policy aims to **reduce risks** for banks._

## 3. US Job Growth Slows Despite Many Openings 

The US job market is showing that job growth has slowed down even though there are many job openings. This means companies are not hiring as much as before. The gap between how many jobs are available and how many people get hired is still a problem. Some experts say this slowdown worries the economy because fewer people working means less money spent. But the White House says the labor market is still on an **upward trajectory**, which means it is slowly getting better. It will be important to watch if hiring picks up in the coming months or stays slow.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **job growth slows** — 일자리 증가가 둔화되다  
>   _The report shows **job growth slows** this month._  
> - **hiring gap** — 채용 격차  
>   _There is a big **hiring gap** between openings and hires._  
> - **upward trajectory** — 상승 궤도, 개선되는 흐름  
>   _Officials hope the labor market follows an **upward trajectory**._

## 4. American Tech Firms Feel Pressure as China Advances in AI 

American technology companies are feeling nervous because China is quickly catching up in the field of artificial intelligence (AI). AI means machines and computers that can learn and think like humans. For years, the US led in AI, but now China is growing fast. This creates competition between the two countries in technology and business. Companies in the US are working hard to keep their top position. This race matters because AI affects many parts of our lives, from healthcare to education. How this competition develops could change global technology leadership.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **sweating bullets** — 몹시 긴장하다, 초조해하다  
>   _Tech companies are **sweating bullets** over the competition._  
> - **catch up** — 따라잡다  
>   _China is trying to **catch up** with the US in AI technology._  
> - **top position** — 최고 위치, 선두 자리  
>   _The US wants to keep its **top position** in AI._
