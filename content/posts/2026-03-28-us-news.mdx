## 1. House GOP Plans Vote on Homeland Security Funding After Senate Bill Rejected  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-28-1.png)

The House Republican leaders want to hold a **vote on** a new Homeland Security funding bill after they **rejected** the Senate’s version. The Senate had already passed a bill to keep the department funded during the government shutdown, but the House GOP found problems with it. This means funding for important security agencies is still uncertain. The vote will decide if the government can continue to pay for border security and immigration enforcement. If the bill does not pass, some government services could stop working. This situation adds pressure on lawmakers to find a solution quickly. The public is watching closely because it affects national safety and government stability.

## 2. This Week’s Immigration News: What’s Happening Now?  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-28-2.png)

This week, there are many updates about immigration in the US and other countries. New policies are being discussed to **vet for cultural compatibility**, meaning officials want to check if immigrants share important cultural values. This idea is part of a bigger debate about how immigration should work. Also, some countries that immigrants pass through on their way to the US are changing their rules, which affects migration routes. These changes may make it harder or easier for people to reach the US. Experts say understanding these policies is important because they affect many families and communities. Immigration stays a key topic in US politics and daily life.

## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Signs of Stability Despite Worker Pessimism  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-28-4.png)

Recent reports say that the number of Americans who feel good about their jobs is going down. This is called **worker thriving** and it is getting weaker. At the same time, the **job market** is showing some strength. Weekly claims for unemployment benefits remain low, which means fewer people are losing jobs. Also, the number of open jobs has risen to about 7 million, which is better than expected. Experts believe the labor market is still **stable**, but many workers feel uncertain about the future. This mix of good and bad news makes it hard to predict what will happen next. People will watch these trends closely for signs of change.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **worker thriving** — 근로자의 성공과 만족도  
>   _The company wants to improve **worker thriving** by offering better benefits._  
> - **job market** — 취업 시장  
>   _The **job market** is very competitive this year._  
> - **stable** — 안정적인  
>   _The economy looks **stable** despite some challenges._  
> - **unemployment claims** — 실업 수당 청구  
>   _The number of **unemployment claims** dropped last week._

## 4. Silicon Valley: The Heart of Tech Innovation and Growth  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-28-3.png)

Silicon Valley is known worldwide as a **tech hub** where new ideas and inventions grow quickly. It is located in California and is home to many famous companies like Apple, Google, and Facebook. The area attracts smart people who want to create the next big thing in technology. This growth helps the US economy and creates many jobs. Recently, political leaders have also asked Silicon Valley billionaires for **policy advice** to improve technology and business. The valley’s influence is very strong in shaping the future of technology worldwide. Many expect it to continue being the center of innovation for years to come.
