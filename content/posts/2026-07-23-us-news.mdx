## 1. Calls for Congressional Hearings on Saudi Nuclear Deal 

A House Democrat is asking Congress to hold official hearings about the Trump administration’s nuclear deal with Saudi Arabia. This deal has raised many questions about safety and politics. People want to know if the deal was made properly and if it could cause problems later. The Democrat believes Congress should examine the details **closely** to protect national security. This issue is important because Saudi Arabia is a key U.S. partner in the Middle East. If Congress finds problems, it could lead to new rules or actions. We will see if these hearings happen and what information comes out next.

## 2. What Trump’s New Public Charge Rule Means for Immigrants 

The Trump administration has a new rule called the “Public Charge” rule. It changes how immigration officials decide if someone can stay in the U.S. The rule looks at if immigrants might need public help, like money or health care. If they do, they could be stopped from getting a green card. This rule is **controversial** because it affects many families and communities. Supporters say it encourages self-reliance, but critics say it hurts vulnerable people. Many immigrants and lawyers are trying to understand how this change will affect them. It’s important because it changes the path to living legally in the U.S.

> - **public charge** — 공공부조 의존자  
>   _The **public charge** rule affects immigration decisions._  
> - **controversial** — 논란이 많은  
>   _The policy is **controversial** in many communities._  
> - **self-reliance** — 자립, 독립  
>   _The government wants to promote **self-reliance** among immigrants._  
> - **vulnerable** — 취약한

## 3. U.S. Job Growth Slows Down in June 

In June, the U.S. added only 57,000 new jobs, which is much less than expected. This shows that the job market is **slowing down**. Many employers are careful about hiring because of economic uncertainty. This slow growth might make it harder for some people to find new jobs or get raises. Experts watch these numbers closely because they show how healthy the economy is. If the trend continues, it could affect wages and spending. Workers and businesses will need to prepare for possible changes in the coming months.

## 4. Why Silicon Valley Keeps Watching China Closely 

Silicon Valley, the center of America’s tech industry, is worried about China’s growing power in technology. American companies watch China **closely** because China is improving fast in areas like artificial intelligence and computer chips. This competition could change the future of technology and business in the U.S. Many Silicon Valley leaders feel they must keep up or risk losing their edge. This situation creates challenges but also opportunities for innovation. Experts say that how the U.S. reacts now will shape the tech world for years to come.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **watch closely** — 면밀히 관찰하다  
>   _Tech firms **watch closely** what their competitors do._  
> - **growing power** — 성장하는 힘  
>   _China’s **growing power** in tech is hard to ignore._  
> - **competition** — 경쟁  
>   _The **competition** between countries drives innovation._  
> - **keep up** — 따라잡다, 뒤쳐지지 않다  
>   _Companies must **keep up** with new technology to survive._
