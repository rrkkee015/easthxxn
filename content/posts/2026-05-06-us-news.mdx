## 1. Trump Pauses Military Effort in Strait of Hormuz ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-05-06-1.png)

The US military has stopped its plan to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, an important waterway for world oil shipments. This decision came after tensions rose between the US and Iran. The Strait is very important because many countries rely on it for oil exports. By pausing, the US is trying to avoid more conflict in the region. Experts say this move shows the US wants to be careful and avoid a big fight. The pause might also give time for talks or new strategies. This is important because any conflict there could affect global oil prices and world peace.

## 2. New US Immigration Rule Helps Doctors Enter the Country ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-05-06-2.png)

The US government has changed immigration rules to make it easier for doctors from other countries to work in the US. This is because there is a big shortage of doctors in many parts of the country. The new rule reverses a previous travel ban that stopped some foreign doctors from coming. With more doctors allowed, hospitals hope to improve patient care and reduce wait times. This change shows how immigration policy can directly affect healthcare. Experts believe it will help solve the problem faster than before. However, the government will still check that all doctors meet US standards.

## 3. US Job Market Shows Mixed Signs in Early 2026 ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-05-06-3.png)

In March, the number of job openings in the US stayed mostly the same, even with conflicts like the situation in Iran. However, companies hired more people in April, showing that many employers want to grow their teams. Some reports say job openings dropped a little, but the number of hires increased sharply. This means more people are finding jobs, but the overall opportunities may be stable or slightly lower. Economists watch these numbers carefully to understand how the economy is doing. A strong job market usually means people feel confident to spend money and businesses feel good about the future.

## 4. The Rise of the “Silicon Valley Right” Changing Tech Culture ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-05-06-4.png)

Silicon Valley is known as the center of technology and innovation in the US. Recently, a new group called the “Silicon Valley Right” is growing. They have different political views compared to the traditional tech culture. This change is affecting how companies operate and what ideas they support. Some say this new group wants more balance in tech’s influence on politics and society. Others worry it might cause divisions inside the tech community. Overall, this shift shows how politics can influence even technology companies and their future direction.
