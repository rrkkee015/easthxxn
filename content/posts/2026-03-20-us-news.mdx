## 1. House GOP Supports $200 Billion Pentagon Budget Boost ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-20-1.png)

The U.S. House Republican leaders recently welcomed a request to increase the Pentagon’s budget by $200 billion. This extra money aims to **jump-start** new military projects and improve national defense. The request is part of a plan called "reconciliation 2.0," which tries to speed up government spending on important programs. This budget boost could **have a big impact** on America's military strength and technology. However, some lawmakers worry about the size of the spending and its effect on the national debt. The Pentagon says this money is needed to keep up with global threats. If approved, the extra funding could **push forward** new weapons and research. The decision will affect future U.S. security and jobs in defense industries.

> - **jump-start** — 빠르게 시작하다, 활성화하다  
>   _The new plan will **jump-start** economic growth._  
> - **have a big impact** — 큰 영향을 미치다  
>   _The budget increase will **have a big impact** on local communities._  
> - **push forward** — 추진하다, 앞으로 나아가다  
>   _We need to **push forward** with the project despite challenges._

## 2. America Needs Fair and Efficient Immigration Courts ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-20-2.png)

A recent policy brief from a leading immigration group explains why America needs better immigration courts. These courts decide the fate of many immigrants who want to live or work in the U.S. The group argues that the current system is too slow and sometimes unfair. They want the courts to be **independent**, meaning free from political influence. They also want them to be **efficient**, so cases can be resolved faster. Improving these courts would help both immigrants and the government. Fair and quick decisions can reduce stress for families and save money. The brief suggests changes that could make the system more just and clear for everyone.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **independent** — 독립적인, 자주적인  
>   _The judges must be **independent** to make fair decisions._  
> - **efficient** — 효율적인  
>   _An **efficient** system saves time and resources._  
> - **reduce stress** — 스트레스를 줄이다  
>   _Good communication can **reduce stress** in the workplace._

## 3. U.S. Employers Cut Jobs as Economy Shows Weak Signs ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-20-3.png)

Recent reports show that U.S. employers have started cutting jobs, which may signal a weaker economy. Companies are letting workers go because they expect less business or want to save money. This change worries many people because job cuts can **shake** the economy. When fewer people have jobs, they spend less money, which can slow down growth. Some experts say this may be a short-term problem, but others fear it could last longer. The government and businesses will watch the job market closely to decide what to do next. Job cuts often affect families and communities deeply.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **cut jobs** — 일자리를 줄이다, 해고하다  
>   _The company had to **cut jobs** due to lower sales._  
> - **shake the economy** — 경제를 흔들다, 불안하게 하다  
>   _A sudden crisis can **shake the economy**._  
> - **watch closely** — 주의 깊게 지켜보다  
>   _Investors **watch closely** for market changes._

## 4. Silicon Valley Profits From War-Related Tech Investments ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-20-4.png)

Silicon Valley companies are seeing profits as they invest heavily in technology linked to war and defense. Many tech firms are making **bets** on military projects, hoping their products will be useful for national security. These bets are **paying off** with big contracts and growing business. The tech industry is known for innovation, and now it is using that skill to help the military. This trend shows how war can change where money flows in the technology world. However, some people worry about the ethical side of selling weapons technology. The future may bring more growth for tech firms that work with defense.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **bet on** — ~에 투자하다, 기대하다  
>   _Investors often **bet on** new technology companies._  
> - **pay off** — 성공하다, 좋은 결과를 낳다  
>   _Hard work will **pay off** in the end._  
> - **growth** — 성장, 발전  
>   _The company is experiencing rapid **growth** this year._
