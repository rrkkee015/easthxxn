## 1. U.S. Plans to Block Strait of Hormuz to Stop Iran’s Toll Payments  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-13-1.png)

The U.S. government announced that it will **blockade** the Strait of Hormuz. This is a very important waterway where ships pass to deliver oil. The U.S. wants to **intercept** ships that pay tolls to Iran. Tolls are fees that ships pay to pass through certain waters. This move comes amid increasing tensions between the U.S. and Iran. The goal is to stop Iran from getting money through these tolls. Experts worry this action could increase conflict in the region. It is a serious step that might affect global oil prices and international relations.

## 2. Trump Administration Takes Legal Action Over Immigration Policy  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-13-2.png)

The Trump administration has filed a lawsuit against Washtenaw County. The government claims the county is **obstructing** immigration policies. To **obstruct** means to block or slow down something. The lawsuit says local officials are not cooperating with federal immigration laws. This is part of a bigger effort to **restore the rule of law** in immigration courts. The administration wants stricter enforcement of immigration rules. The case shows how immigration remains a controversial topic in the U.S. Many expect the courts to decide how much power local governments have in immigration matters.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **obstruct** — 방해하다, 막다  
>   _The protesters tried to **obstruct** the road._  
> - **file a lawsuit** — 소송을 제기하다  
>   _The company decided to **file a lawsuit** against the contractor._  
> - **restore the rule of law** — 법치주의를 회복하다  
>   _The new government promised to **restore the rule of law**._  
> - **enforcement** — 집행, 시행  
>   _Stronger **enforcement** of traffic laws reduces accidents._  
> - **cooperate** — 협력하다  
>   _The two teams **cooperate** to finish the project._

## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Signs of Recovery in March  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-13-3.png)

The U.S. labor market improved in March as employers added 178,000 jobs. This is good news after months of slow growth. A stronger **job market** means more people have work and can earn money. The unemployment rate stayed steady, showing balance between jobs and workers. Experts say this growth could help the economy grow faster. However, some industries still face challenges. The report helps government and businesses decide policies to support workers. Many Americans watch these numbers closely because jobs affect daily life.

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces Pressure as AI Changes the Tech World  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-13-4.png)

Silicon Valley, the center of U.S. technology, is going through big changes because of artificial intelligence (AI). AI is a powerful tool that can **change the world** in many ways. But first, it is **changing Silicon Valley** itself. Many companies are racing to develop better AI technology. At the same time, other countries like China are trying to attract top AI experts from Silicon Valley. This creates competition and pressure for U.S. tech companies. Experts say the U.S. must act fast to keep its leadership in AI. How Silicon Valley adapts will affect the future of technology and business worldwide.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **change the world** — 세상을 바꾸다  
>   _New inventions can **change the world**._  
> - **pressure** — 압력, 부담  
>   _She feels **pressure** to finish the project on time._  
> - **race to develop** — 개발 경쟁을 벌이다  
>   _Companies **race to develop** the latest smartphone._  
> - **adapt** — 적응하다  
>   _We must **adapt** to new technology quickly._  
> - **leadership** — 지도력, 선도  
>   _The company shows strong **leadership** in the market._
