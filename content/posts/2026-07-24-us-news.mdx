## 1. New US Tariffs Target Forced Labor in Global Trade  


The Trump administration recently announced new tariffs on products from dozens of countries. The government says these tariffs are to fight **forced labor**, which means people working against their will in bad conditions. This move aims to stop goods made with forced labor from entering the US market. It is important because it affects trade relationships and might cause prices to change for some products. Companies will need to check where their products come from more carefully. The government hopes this will encourage better labor practices worldwide. In the future, some countries might negotiate to remove the tariffs by proving they do not use forced labor.

## 2. How New Rules Affect Immigrants in the US  


The US government recently updated rules about immigration, especially the **public charge** rule. This rule looks at whether immigrants might use government help like health care or money support. If they do, it could make it harder for them to get a visa or stay in the country. The rule is important because many immigrants worry about asking for help when they need it. Experts say strict policies can also affect immigrants’ mental health. Some groups want the government to make the rules easier so immigrants can feel safer and healthier. These changes might affect many families living in the US now or planning to come.

## 3. Many Americans Stay in Jobs Just for Health Insurance  


A recent survey shows that about one in four American workers feels **locked in** their jobs because of health insurance. This means they stay at a job even if they want to leave, just to keep their medical coverage. Health insurance in the US is often tied to employment, so losing a job can mean losing insurance. This situation can cause stress and stop people from finding better jobs. Experts say this affects the **job market** and worker happiness. Some suggest changes to make health care separate from jobs. This issue is important as it affects millions of workers and their families.

> - **locked in** — 꼼짝 못하는, 묶여 있는

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces Pressure From China's Tech Advances  


Silicon Valley, the center of US technology, is feeling **pressure** as China grows stronger in areas like artificial intelligence and computer chips. American companies watch China closely because of trade tensions and competition. This situation is sometimes called a **tech race** between the two countries. Some US businesses worry about losing their lead in new technologies. The government is also involved, creating rules to protect technology secrets. This rivalry may change how companies work and where they build products in the future.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **pressure** — 압박, 부담  
>   _Tech companies feel **pressure** to stay ahead of competitors._  
> - **tech race** — 기술 경쟁  
>   _The **tech race** between countries drives innovation and tension._  
> - **trade tension** — 무역 긴장

> - **protect technology secrets** — 기술 비밀을 보호하다
