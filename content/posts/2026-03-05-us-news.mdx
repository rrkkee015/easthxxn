## 1. Senate Rejects Resolution to End Iran Strikes ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-05-1.png)

The US Senate recently voted against a resolution that would force former President Trump to stop military strikes in Iran. The resolution aimed to limit the president’s power to start or continue attacks without Congress's approval. Many senators believe that Congress should have a bigger role in deciding about war. However, others argued that quick action is sometimes necessary for national security. This vote shows the ongoing debate about how much control the president should have over military decisions. The outcome means that Trump and future presidents may still act without needing direct permission from Congress. This issue is important because it affects how the US handles conflicts and peace efforts in the Middle East.

## 2. Trump 2.0’s First Year Reshaping US Immigration ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-05-2.png)

In his first year back in office, Trump has changed many rules about legal immigration in the US. His policies focus on reducing the number of people who can enter the country legally. This includes stricter rules for visas and more limits on who can stay. Some people support these changes because they believe it protects American jobs and security. Others think the policies are too harsh and hurt families and workers. The immigration system in the US is very important because it affects millions of people’s lives. The situation will likely continue to change as new leaders decide how to handle immigration.

## 3. US Job Market: 63,000 New Jobs Added but Growth is Sluggish ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-05-3.png)

In February, US businesses added 63,000 new jobs, according to ADP. While this shows some hiring, experts say the job market is still slow and not very strong. Some industries are hiring more workers, but others are cutting jobs. Many employees are holding on tightly to their current jobs because they are worried about the future. This trend is important because jobs affect the economy and people’s daily lives. If hiring stays slow, it could mean people have less money to spend. Government leaders watch these numbers closely to decide if they need to help the economy improve.

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces Challenges as It Prepares for War ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-05-4.png)

Silicon Valley, known for technology innovation, is now involved in helping the US prepare for possible conflicts. Tech companies are working on new defense technologies and supporting the military. Some leaders warn that the region has ignored risks like problems with Taiwan’s chip production. Taiwan makes many important computer chips used worldwide. If there is a disaster or conflict there, it could hurt the tech industry and the economy. This situation shows that technology companies must think about global politics and security as part of their future plans.
