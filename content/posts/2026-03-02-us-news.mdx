Former President Trump says he supports **legal immigration**, but many experts say his policies tell a different story. While he talks about welcoming immigrants who follow the rules, some of his rules have made it harder for people to enter the US legally. This has caused confusion and frustration among immigrants and lawmakers. Immigration is a big topic in US politics because it affects the economy and communities. The debate shows how words and actions can sometimes **contradict** each other. Many people want clearer and fairer immigration laws in the future.

California’s latest job report shows a mix of **job openings** and workers quitting their jobs. The report, published by the Bureau of Labor Statistics, talks about labor turnover, which means how many people leave jobs and how many new jobs are available. The data can help businesses and workers understand the job market better. Some industries are growing and need more workers, while others are slower. Knowing these trends helps people plan their careers and job searches. The employment rate still changes month by month, so experts watch these reports carefully.

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces Taiwan Chip Crisis  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-02-4.png)  

Silicon Valley has long relied on chips made in Taiwan, but experts warn about a **looming disaster** if the supply chain breaks down. Taiwan makes many important computer chips used worldwide. If there is a problem like a natural disaster, political conflict, or factory issue, the chip supply could stop. This would hurt many tech companies and the global economy. The article says Silicon Valley has ignored this risk for too long. Now, some companies and governments want to find solutions to protect the chip supply. This issue is very important for the future of technology.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **looming disaster** — 임박한 재앙, 다가오는 큰 문제  
>   _Experts warn of a **looming disaster** if the chip supply stops._  
> - **supply chain** — 공급망, 유통망  
>   _The **supply chain** connects factories to customers._  
> - **break down** — 고장 나다, 무너지다  
>   _If the system **breaks down**, production will stop._  
> - **ignored** — 무시된, 간과된  
>   _The warning signs were **ignored** for many years._
