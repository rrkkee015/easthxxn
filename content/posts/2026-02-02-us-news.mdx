## 1. Father and Son Return Home After ICE Detention ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-02-02-1.png)

A 5-year-old boy named Liam Conejo Ramos and his father were recently released from an ICE facility in Texas. They have now returned safely to their home in Minnesota. This story is important because it shows the challenges immigrant families face when separated by immigration policies. The father and son were separated for some time, which caused stress and worry. Their return brings relief, but many families still face similar situations. This case highlights the ongoing debate about how immigration laws affect families. It also raises questions about what can be done to keep families together during legal processes.

## 2. Bomb Cyclone Brings Bitter Cold to the Southeast ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-02-02-2.png)

A strong storm called a "bomb cyclone" recently hit the Southeast of the United States. It brought very cold temperatures and heavy snow to areas that usually have mild winters. This weather event surprised many people and caused problems like road closures and power outages. The cold weather is expected to last for several days, making it hard for people to go outside or travel. Experts warn that such storms may become more common because of climate changes. The storm also shows how weather can have a big effect on daily life and safety. People are advised to stay warm and be careful while driving.

## 3. US Job Market Shows Signs of Struggle in 2025 ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-02-02-3.png)

The US job market is showing some difficulties this year. Reports say that layoffs are happening at levels similar to the pandemic times. At the same time, job growth is expected to be slow and "uncomfortably" weak. This means it might take a long time for the market to get better. Many people are worried about finding or keeping jobs in this tough environment. Experts suggest that workers should improve their skills and stay flexible to deal with changes. Employers may also need to adjust their hiring plans. Overall, the job market is uncertain, and people should prepare for challenges ahead.

## 4. Silicon Valley Tech Workers Campaign Against ICE in US Cities ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-02-02-4.png)

Tech workers in Silicon Valley are actively campaigning to remove ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) from US cities. They believe ICE’s presence harms immigrant communities and creates fear. These workers are using their influence to push for change in immigration policies. This movement shows how technology professionals are getting involved in social and political issues. It is part of a larger debate about the role of tech companies and workers in society. The campaign could lead to new policies that protect immigrants better. It also highlights the power of workers to affect important public matters.

> **actively campaigning to** — 적극적으로 ~하기 위해 캠페인하다
