## 1. U.S. Job Growth Slows Down in June 

The United States added only 57,000 new jobs in June, which is much lower than experts expected. This **snaps** a long **hot streak** of strong job growth. Many people thought the job market would continue to do well, but this shows that hiring is **slowing down**. Economists say this could mean that companies are being careful about hiring because of economic uncertainty. The unemployment rate stayed low, so people who want jobs can still find them. But the small number of new jobs might affect how the government decides on interest rates soon. Many are watching closely to see if this trend will continue or change next month.

## 2. Changes in U.S. Immigration Policy Affecting Spouses 

Under the Trump administration, there were many changes in U.S. immigration rules, especially for spouses of American citizens. These changes made it harder and slower for spouses to get green cards and live with their families in the U.S. The new rules included more interviews and paperwork, which caused delays. Many families felt stressed because they had to wait a long time to be together. Some of these policies are still in place today, and experts say they have a big effect on immigrant families. Immigration officials say they want to keep the system safe but also fair. Many are hoping for new laws to make the process easier in the future.

## 3. Latin America’s Tech City Attracts Billions in Investment 

A city in Latin America is becoming a new **Silicon Valley**. It has attracted more than $2.5 billion in tech investments recently. This growth shows that Latin America is becoming important in the global technology world. Many startup companies are opening offices there, especially in software and internet services. The city offers good talent and lower costs compared to the United States. Investors see it as a place with many opportunities. This boom could help the region create more jobs and improve its economy. Experts say the city might become a leader in technology for Latin America.

## 4. Belgium Ends Team USA’s World Cup Run Amid Political Firestorm 

In a surprising World Cup moment, Belgium defeated Team USA and ended their chance to win the tournament. This loss came at a time of political **firestorm** in the U.S. about sports and national pride. Many fans and politicians talked about the game and what it means for the country. Some believe the team showed strong effort, while others criticized decisions made during the game. The political discussions show how sports and politics sometimes mix. The team will now look forward to the next World Cup and hope to improve. Many say this event shows how sports can bring people together and also create debates.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **firestorm** — 큰 논란, 격렬한 논쟁

> - **run** (in sports) — 경기 진행, 진출
