## 1. Iran’s President Apologizes Amid Middle East Tensions ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-08-1.png)

The president of Iran recently **apologized** to Gulf countries to calm tensions after threats of more attacks from former President Trump. This comes during a time of serious conflict in the Middle East. Iran wanted to show it respects its neighbors, hoping to avoid more violence. However, Trump warned that the U.S. might launch additional **strikes** if needed. This situation is important because it affects global peace and oil supplies. Many countries are watching closely to see if this apology will lead to calmer relations or more conflict. The future is uncertain, but the apology is a sign that Iran wants to reduce tensions for now.

## 3. US Job Market Shows Signs of Worry in February ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-08-3.png)

In February, the U.S. job market showed unexpected problems. The country actually lost 92,000 jobs, surprising many experts who expected growth. This is worrying because jobs are very important for the economy and people’s lives. Some industries like healthcare are still strong, but others are slowing down. This drop may affect spending, business confidence, and future hiring. People will watch carefully to see if this is a short-term problem or a sign of a bigger economic slowdown. The government may take action to help the economy recover.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **job market** — 취업 시장  
>   _The **job market** can change quickly._  
> - **unexpected** — 예상치 못한  
>   _The results were **unexpected** and surprising._  
> - **lose jobs** — 일자리를 잃다  
>   _Many workers **lose jobs** during a recession._  
> - **sign of** — ~의 신호  
>   _This is a **sign of** trouble in the economy._

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces a Taiwan Chip Crisis ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-08-4.png)

Silicon Valley is facing a big problem with computer chips. Most chips come from factories in Taiwan, and there is a risk of a disaster if something happens there. This is dangerous because many tech companies depend on these chips for their products. The article says Silicon Valley has ignored this risk for too long. Now, companies and government leaders are trying to find new ways to protect chip supplies. This crisis shows how important technology and global connections are. It also means that the tech industry needs to be ready for unexpected problems.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **face a problem** — 문제에 직면하다  
>   _The company **faces a problem** with supply shortages._  
> - **depend on** — ~에 의존하다  
>   _We **depend on** computers for work._  
> - **ignore risk** — 위험을 무시하다  
>   _Some people **ignore risk**, but that can be dangerous._  
> - **protect supplies** — 공급을 보호하다  
>   _We must **protect supplies** to avoid shortages._
