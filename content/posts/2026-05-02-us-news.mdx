## 1. Trump Plans to Reduce U.S. Military Presence in Germany  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-05-02-1.png)

The U.S. government announced a plan to **downsize** its military forces in Germany. This means the number of American troops stationed there will go down. The decision is part of a new strategy to change how the U.S. defends itself in Europe. Officials say this move will save money and focus efforts in other areas. Some experts worry it might weaken the U.S. position in Europe. Germany is an important ally, so this change could affect future cooperation. The government will carefully decide which troops return home and which stay. People are watching closely to see how this shift will impact global security.

## 2. This Week’s Important Immigration News  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-05-02-2.png)

These changes affect visas, work permits, and border policies. Immigration officers continue to carry out raids and deportations, which causes protests in many cities. Immigration remains a complex and important issue in the U.S. These updates help us understand what is happening and what might come next. ## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Strong Growth in March  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-05-02-3.png)

The U.S. economy added 178,000 new jobs last month. This was a much better number than expected after a slow February. More people found work in areas like health care, education, and technology. However, experts warn this **hiring boomlet** might not continue for long. Some parts of the country still face high unemployment or slow growth. The job market is important because it shows how well the economy is doing. Many Americans rely on these new jobs to improve their lives. We will watch closely to see if this positive trend lasts.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Political Shift Toward Conservatism  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-05-02-4.png)

Silicon Valley, known for its liberal views, is now seeing a rise of conservative ideas. Some workers and leaders in tech companies are changing their political beliefs. This new group is sometimes called the “Silicon Valley Right.” They want different policies on issues like free speech and business regulations. This change is surprising because the tech world used to strongly support progressive causes. The shift could affect how tech companies act and what products they create. It also shows how politics can change even in places we expect to be one way. Experts say this could lead to new debates inside the industry.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **political shift** — 정치적 변화  
>   _The country experienced a **political shift** after the election._  
> - **conservatism** — 보수주의  
>   _Some cities are seeing a rise in **conservatism**._  
> - **progressive** — 진보적인  
>   _Many tech companies support **progressive** ideas._  
> - **debate** — 토론  
>   _There is a big **debate** about technology and privacy._
