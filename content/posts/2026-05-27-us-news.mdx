## 1. Texas Election Surprises Change Political Landscape 

In Texas, recent runoff elections brought some major surprises. Senator John Cornyn lost his seat, which shocked many voters and politicians. This election is important because Texas is a large state with a lot of influence in national politics. The results show that voters want some changes in leadership and ideas. These changes could affect laws about education, health care, and voting rights. The election also showed strong voter participation, meaning many people care about the future of Texas. Experts say this might change how both parties plan for upcoming national elections. It is clear the political scene in Texas is **shifting** fast.

## 2. New Visa Rules Could Force Some to Leave U.S. Before Green Card 

The U.S. government announced a new rule for people who want green cards. Now, many visa holders, especially in states like Georgia, must return to their home countries to apply for a green card. This is a big change from before, when some could apply while staying in the U.S. This rule could cause stress and uncertainty for families and workers. The government says this will help improve the immigration process, but critics worry it will cause delays and hardship. Many visa holders are trying to understand how this affects their future plans. This policy shows how immigration rules can **impact** people's lives in big ways.

## 3. Teen Summer Job Market Hits Toughest Time in 78 Years 

This summer, teenagers in the U.S. are facing the hardest job market in nearly 80 years. Many teens want summer jobs, but there are fewer openings and more competition. Experts say the economy is changing, and some companies are hiring fewer young workers. This makes it harder for teens to get work experience and earn money during school breaks. Some teens feel frustrated because the “American dream” of working and gaining independence seems more difficult now. Parents and schools are looking for new ways to help young people find jobs. This trend might continue if the economy stays uncertain.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Tech Boom Faces New Challenges 

Silicon Valley, known for its fast-growing tech companies, is now facing a difficult time. The rapid rise of new technology changed the Bay Area’s economy and culture. But now, many tech workers say it is **brutal** to find jobs because of changes in the market and the rise of artificial intelligence (AI). Some companies are cutting jobs or hiring less. This shift is surprising because Silicon Valley was known for constant growth. Experts think this might lead to a new phase where tech firms work more carefully and focus on different skills. The future of the tech industry in this famous region is changing quickly.
