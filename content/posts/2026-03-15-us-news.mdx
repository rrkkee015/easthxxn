## 1. Rising Tensions: U.S. and Allies May Send War Ships to Strait of Hormuz  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-15-1.png)

Recently, former President Trump claimed that an international coalition will send war ships to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. This strait is a very important waterway for oil shipping. Some countries have blocked it, which causes worry for the global oil supply. Trump said this plan would help keep the strait open and safe for all ships. The situation is tense because it involves Iran and other countries with strong military forces. If the coalition sends ships, it might increase the chances of conflict but also could protect important trade routes. People are watching closely to see what will happen next.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **international coalition** — 국제 연합체 (두 개 이상의 나라가 함께 하는 단체)  
>   _The **international coalition** worked together to solve the crisis._  
> - **reopen the strait** — 해협을 다시 열다  
>   _The navy plans to **reopen the strait** to allow ships to pass._  
> - **keep the strait open** — 해협을 열어두다  
>   _It is important to **keep the strait open** for trade._  
> - **increase the chances** — 가능성을 높이다  
>   _This action could **increase the chances** of peace._  
> - **trade routes** — 무역 경로  
>   _Many goods travel along important **trade routes**._

## 2. Divided Opinions: Americans See Immigration Very Differently  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-15-2.png)

A recent national survey shows a big gap in how Americans think about immigration. Many people have very different opinions depending on their political views. Some support legal immigration and believe immigrants help the country. Others are worried about illegal immigration and want stricter rules. The survey calls this a “partisan chasm,” which means a strong division between political groups. This division makes it hard to find common solutions for immigration policies. Immigration is a very important topic because it affects the economy, culture, and communities. Leaders may need to work harder to bring people together.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **national survey** — 전국 조사  
>   _The **national survey** collected data from thousands of people._  
> - **big gap** — 큰 차이  
>   _There is a **big gap** between the opinions of the two groups._  
> - **partisan chasm** — 당파 간 큰 분열  
>   _The **partisan chasm** makes it difficult to agree on laws._  
> - **legal immigration** — 합법 이민  
>   _Many support **legal immigration** to help the economy._  
> - **stricter rules** — 더 엄격한 규칙  
>   _Some want **stricter rules** to control borders._

## 3. Job Openings Increase, But Hiring Does Not Follow  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-15-3.png)

In the U.S., job openings recently jumped to a three-month high. This means companies have many open positions they want to fill. However, businesses are not actually hiring more people right now. This strange situation shows that companies may be cautious about adding new workers. Some possible reasons include worries about the economy or changes in business needs. This gap between openings and hiring can affect workers and job seekers. It might also influence how the economy grows in the next months. Experts are watching this carefully to understand the real job market.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **job openings** — 일자리 공석  
>   _There are many **job openings** in the city._  
> - **jump to a high** — 갑자기 높아지다  
>   _Sales **jumped to a high** last month._  
> - **not actually hiring** — 실제로 고용하지 않다  
>   _The company has openings but is **not actually hiring** yet._  
> - **cautious about** — ~에 대해 조심하는  
>   _They are **cautious about** spending money now._  
> - **job market** — 구직 시장  
>   _The **job market** is changing quickly._

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces a Growing Threat from Taiwan Chip Supply  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-15-4.png)

Silicon Valley is famous as a global tech hub. It is home to many important companies and innovations. However, there is a big issue that Silicon Valley has ignored for a long time. Taiwan produces a large part of the world’s computer chips, which are essential for tech devices. If something bad happens in Taiwan, it could cause a disaster for chip supplies. This would affect many tech companies in Silicon Valley and beyond. Experts warn that more attention and action are needed to avoid a serious problem. The future of technology depends on solving this supply risk.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **tech hub** — 기술 중심지  
>   _Silicon Valley is a famous **tech hub**._  
> - **ignored for a long time** — 오랫동안 무시되다  
>   _This problem was **ignored for a long time**._  
> - **computer chips** — 컴퓨터 칩  
>   _**Computer chips** are inside many electronic devices._  
> - **supply risk** — 공급 위험  
>   _The company faces a **supply risk** if parts don’t arrive._  
> - **serious problem** — 심각한 문제  
>   _We need to find a solution to this **serious problem**._
