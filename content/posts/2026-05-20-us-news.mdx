## 1. Trump’s Influence Ends Congressman Massie’s Career in GOP Primary  
  

Rep. Thomas Massie lost his Republican primary election, showing how strong former President Trump’s influence still is in American politics. Massie was known for speaking his mind, but Trump’s support went to a different candidate. This loss is part of a bigger trend where Trump’s backing can **make or break** politicians in GOP races. Many politicians who oppose Trump face challenges in winning primaries. This event matters because it shows how divided the Republican Party is and how Trump shapes its future. Massie’s defeat may encourage other Republicans to follow Trump’s lead or risk losing their seats. It also affects the balance of power in Congress and the 2024 election strategies.

> - **backing** — 지지  
>   _The candidate gained important **backing** from party leaders._  
> - **divide** — 나누다, 분열시키다

## 2. Important Facts About Immigrants’ Health Coverage in the US  
  

Many immigrants in the US face challenges in getting good health coverage. A new report from KFF explains key facts about which immigrants have health insurance and which do not. The report shows that legal immigrants have better coverage than those without papers. Still, many immigrants worry about the cost of medical care. Health coverage is important because it helps people get treatment and avoid big medical bills. The US government and organizations are working to improve immigrants’ access to health services. Understanding these facts can help us see the real problems immigrants face. It also shows why health policy is an important part of immigration discussions.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **health coverage** — 건강 보험  
>   _Access to **health coverage** is essential for everyone._  
> - **face challenges** — 어려움에 직면하다  
>   _Many immigrants **face challenges** when applying for insurance._  
> - **legal immigrants** — 합법 이민자  
>   _**Legal immigrants** often have more options for health care._  
> - **access to** — ~에 대한 접근  
>   _Improving **access to** health services is a government goal._

## 3. AI Technology Is Changing Jobs and Helping Blue-Collar Workers  
  

Artificial intelligence (AI) is changing the American job market in big ways. Unlike some ideas that AI takes jobs away, many blue-collar workers are set to **benefit from** new AI tools. These workers, who do manual or skilled trade jobs, can use AI to improve their work and earn more. The AI economy is making new opportunities for training and better pay. Experts say this shift could help many workers achieve the American Dream in a new way. However, the change also means workers need to learn new skills to keep up. This story shows that technology does not only threaten jobs but can also create growth. It highlights the need for education and support for all workers.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **benefit from** — ~로부터 이익을 얻다  
>   _Many workers **benefit from** new AI technology._  
> - **blue-collar workers** — 육체 노동자  
>   _**Blue-collar workers** often work in factories or construction._  
> - **set to** — ~할 준비가 된  
>   _These jobs are **set to** change with the help of AI._  
> - **achieve the American Dream** — 미국식 성공을 이루다  
>   _Technology can help people **achieve the American Dream**._

## 4. “Silicon Valley Right” Influences the Future of Tech Politics  
  

A new political group called the “Silicon Valley Right” is changing the technology world in the US. This group supports more conservative ideas compared to the tech industry’s usual liberal views. They want a stronger focus on national security and less influence from China in tech business. This shift affects how companies develop new technology and work with the government. The rise of this group shows that politics in Silicon Valley is not one-sided anymore. It also reflects bigger tensions between the US and China over technology leadership. This change could affect innovation, investments, and US tech policies in the near future.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **political group** — 정치 단체  
>   _The **political group** has new ideas about technology._  
> - **conservative ideas** — 보수적인 생각  
>   _Some people in tech now support more **conservative ideas**._  
> - **national security** — 국가 안보  
>   _They want to protect **national security** in technology._  
> - **one-sided** — 편파적인, 한쪽으로 치우친  
>   _Politics in Silicon Valley is not **one-sided** anymore._
