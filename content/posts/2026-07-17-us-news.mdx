## 1. Trump Speaks on Elections and Voter Data Security 

Former President Trump gave a **primetime** speech about the US elections. He talked about concerns over election fairness and voter data. At the same time, the White House said China might have had **access to voter data**. This claim has raised alarms about election security and foreign interference. Election experts say this issue is serious because voter data helps run elections smoothly and safely. If the data is **compromised**, it could affect many people’s votes. The government is working to find out more about these claims. This story shows how important it is to protect election information from outside threats.

## 3. Millions Locked Out of a Better Job Market 

Even though the US job market is **improving**, about two million workers are still **locked out**. This means they cannot find work or get good jobs. Employers say there are many open jobs, but some workers face challenges like lack of skills or transportation. The job growth is **steady but slower** than before. Some experts worry about the **hiring gap**—the difference between job openings and people getting hired. This situation can make it hard for the economy to grow faster. Policymakers are looking for ways to help these workers join the workforce again.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **locked out** — 배제된, 일자리를 구하지 못하는  
>   _Many workers feel **locked out** of the job market._  
> - **steady but slower** — 꾸준하지만 느린  
>   _Hiring is **steady but slower** this month._  
> - **hiring gap** — 채용 간극, 일자리 공석과 채용 사이 차이  
>   _The **hiring gap** shows some jobs remain unfilled._
