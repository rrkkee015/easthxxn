## 1. US Strikes Iran Targets Again After Recent Attacks  
  

The US military has launched air strikes on targets linked to Iran for the second time in just a few days. These strikes came after attacks on US forces in the Middle East, which raised tensions between the two countries. The US says these actions are **necessary** to defend its troops and interests in the region. Iran has warned the US not to continue with these attacks, but the situation remains tense. Experts worry this could lead to a bigger conflict if both sides keep reacting. The world is watching closely to see what happens next. These events show how fragile peace can be in important parts of the world.

## 2. New Rules May Force Green Card Applicants to Wait Abroad  
  

The US government has introduced new guidance that could make many people applying for green cards wait outside the country. This change means applicants might have to leave the US and apply from their home countries. Officials say this is to improve the application process, but many people and experts call it confusing and difficult. This new rule could **cloud the path** to getting a green card, which means it will be harder to know what will happen. For immigrants hoping to live and work in the US, this is a big challenge. The new policy could slow down family reunions and work opportunities. Many are waiting to see if there will be more changes soon.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **guidance** — 안내, 지침  
>   _The teacher gave us clear **guidance** on how to complete the project._  
> - **force someone to** — ~하도록 강요하다  
>   _The bad weather **forced** us **to** cancel the trip._  
> - **cloud the path** — 길을 흐리게 하다, 불확실하게 만들다  
>   _The new rules **cloud the path** to success._  
> - **application process** — 신청 절차  
>   _The **application process** for a visa can take several months._  
> - **slow down** — 느리게 하다  
>   _Traffic jams **slow down** the journey._

## 3. California Leads Nation with Big Job Growth  
  

California added 128,600 new jobs in one year, a much higher number than the rest of the United States, which added only about 11,700 jobs. This surprising growth shows California’s economy is getting stronger even when other parts of the country struggle. Experts say this is because California has many industries like technology, entertainment, and agriculture. More jobs mean more opportunities for people to work and earn money. However, some worry about housing and the cost of living as more people find jobs there. This job growth might help the whole US economy in the future. California’s success could be a model for other states to follow.

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces New Challenges After Big Changes  
  

Silicon Valley, known as the center of technology and innovation in California, is facing important changes. Many big tech companies have laid off workers recently, and some startups are changing their plans. This shift is affecting the whole Bay Area economy and how people live there. Some experts say the tech boom that shaped Silicon Valley might be slowing down. New ideas and business models will be needed to keep the area strong. Workers who lost jobs are looking for new places to work, and some companies are trying to help them. The future of Silicon Valley is still uncertain but very important for the US tech world.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습

> - **startup** — 신생 기업

> - **shift** — 변화, 이동
