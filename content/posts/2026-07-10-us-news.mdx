## 1. Iran Claims Attack on U.S. Military Targets in Gulf 

Iran has said it **hit** U.S. military targets in the Gulf region. This action follows the death of a major Iranian leader, Khamenei. The attack shows rising tensions between the two countries, which have been in conflict for years. The U.S. has not confirmed the damage. Experts say this incident could **lead to** more conflict in the area. Many are watching closely to see how the U.S. will respond. This news is important because it affects global security and oil shipping routes.

## 2. Federal Government Sues Maryland Over Immigration Policies 

The federal government has **sued** Maryland because of the state’s immigration sanctuary policies. These policies protect immigrants from being arrested just for their immigration status. The government says Maryland is not following federal immigration laws. Maryland argues it is protecting communities and public safety. This case is part of a bigger debate about immigration in the U.S. The outcome could **affect** how states manage immigration rules in the future.

> - **sue** — 고소하다, 소송을 제기하다  
>   _The company was **sued** for breaking the contract._  
> - **sanctuary policies** — 난민·이민자 보호 정책  
>   _Some cities have **sanctuary policies** to protect immigrants._  
> - **federal** — 연방의

## 3. Job Growth Slows as U.S. Employers Hesitate to Hire 

In June, U.S. employers were **reluctant** to add many new jobs. Hiring slowed down more than experts thought it would. Some say the slow hiring is because of worries about the economy. Despite this, the White House says the labor market is still on an **upward trajectory**, meaning it is improving over time. The slower job growth could **signal** challenges ahead for workers. This news matters because jobs are important for people’s incomes and the overall economy.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **reluctant** — 꺼리는, 주저하는  
>   _Many people are **reluctant** to change jobs during uncertain times._  
> - **upward trajectory** — 상승세  
>   _The company’s sales are on an **upward trajectory**._  
> - **signal** — 신호를 보내다, 알리다  
>   _The data **signals** a possible slowdown in growth._  
> - **labor market** — 노동 시장  
>   _The **labor market** shows signs of improvement._

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Shift Toward Conservative Politics 

Silicon Valley, known for its technology companies, is seeing a rise in conservative political ideas. This new group is sometimes called the “Silicon Valley Right.” Many tech leaders and workers are changing their views. They say they want more balance between politics and business. This change could **reshape** how technology companies work and how they deal with government rules. It is interesting because Silicon Valley has been mostly liberal before. People want to see how this shift will affect the tech world.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **shift** — 변화, 이동  
>   _There is a **shift** in public opinion._  
> - **conservative** — 보수적인  
>   _Some voters have **conservative** views._  
> - **reshape** — 재구성하다, 바꾸다  
>   _New laws can **reshape** the economy._  
> - **balance** — 균형  
>   _We need a **balance** between work and rest._
