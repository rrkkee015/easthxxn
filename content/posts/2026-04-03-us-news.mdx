## 1. Trump Changes Top U.S. Attorney General Position ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-03-1.png)

President Trump has **ousted** Pam Bondi from her role as attorney general. This means he removed her from the position, which is a powerful legal job in the government. The attorney general helps lead the Justice Department and gives advice on legal matters. This change comes during a time when Trump is trying to make big changes in government leadership. It may affect how the country handles laws and investigations in the coming months. People are watching closely because this role influences important decisions about law and order. The new attorney general will likely support Trump’s policies more strongly.

## 2. Iran Threatens Major U.S. Tech Companies ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-03-4.png)

Iran has issued a strong warning to U.S. tech companies like Meta, Google, and Apple. They said, “From now on,” meaning they plan to take action against these companies. This threat comes because of political tensions between the U.S. and Iran. If Iran follows through, it could affect how these companies work in the region. It also shows how technology and politics connect in the modern world. These companies are important because many people use their products every day. The situation may lead to more conflicts or new rules for tech businesses.

> - **political tensions** — 정치적 긴장

## 3. U.S. Job Growth Slows in March ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-03-3.png)

According to Revelio Public Labor Statistics, the U.S. gained 19,400 jobs in March. This number is smaller than many expected. Job growth is important because it shows how well the economy is doing. When more people have jobs, they can spend money and help businesses grow. However, experts warn that the ongoing war in Ukraine could **hit hiring** and slow down the job market. People will watch next reports carefully to see if this slow growth continues. Employers may become more careful about hiring new workers soon.

## 4. Immigration Policies Affect School Access for Children

New federal and state rules are making it harder for immigrant children to get good education. These policies often target immigrant families and can cause fear in schools. Experts say this hurts decades of progress in education access for all kids. When children feel unsafe or unwelcome, they may not attend school regularly or do their best. This issue is important because education helps children succeed in life. Many people hope policies will change to protect immigrant children’s rights and support their learning.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **policies** — 정책  
>   _New **policies** affect how schools operate._  
> - **education access** — 교육 기회  
>   _Everyone should have equal **education access**._  
> - **progress** — 발전, 진전  
>   _There has been a lot of **progress** in health care._  
> - **target** — 목표로 삼다, 겨냥하다
