## 1. New Homeland Security Secretary Takes Office ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-24-1.png)

Markwayne Mullin was recently confirmed as the new secretary of Homeland Security in the United States. Homeland Security is an important government department that protects the country from threats like terrorism, cyberattacks, and natural disasters. Mullin will lead efforts to keep the country safe during a time of many challenges. His work will include managing border security and preparing for emergencies. This change in leadership comes at a time when security issues are very high on the national agenda. People expect Mullin to bring strong ideas to improve safety. The government hopes his leadership will make a positive difference soon.

## 2. Job Market Shows Unexpected Losses ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-24-2.png)

The United States recently lost 92,000 jobs, which surprised many experts. Usually, the job market grows or stays stable, but this unexpected loss worries people about the economy. Jobs are important because they help people earn money and keep the economy moving. When many people lose jobs, it can slow down spending and business activity. Experts are watching closely to see if this is a short-term problem or a sign of bigger issues ahead. The government and companies may need to act to improve the situation soon. This news affects millions of workers and their families.

## 3. US Immigration Policies Affect Transit Countries ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-24-3.png)

US immigration policies are not only important for people entering the United States. They also affect countries where migrants travel through on their way to the US. These “transit countries” sometimes face challenges because of changes in US rules. For example, stricter policies can cause more people to stay longer in these countries, creating pressure on local resources and governments. Experts study these effects to understand how immigration policies impact not just the US but also other nations. This knowledge helps policymakers create better immigration solutions. The situation is complex and will likely continue to change in the coming years.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **immigration policies** — 이민 정책  
>   _The government changed its **immigration policies** recently._  
> - **transit countries** — 경유국, 중간 국가  
>   _Many migrants stay in **transit countries** before reaching their final destination._  
> - **face challenges** — 어려움에 직면하다  
>   _Small businesses often **face challenges** during economic changes._  
> - **put pressure on** — ~에 압박을 가하다  
>   _The new project will **put pressure on** the team’s schedule._  
> - **create better solutions** — 더 나은 해결책을 만들다  
>   _We need to **create better solutions** for traffic problems._

## 4. US and China Differ but Can Collaborate on AI ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-24-4.png)

The US and China have different views on artificial intelligence (AI), but experts say they can still work together. AI is a powerful technology that can change many parts of life, like medicine, business, and security. The two countries often disagree on rules and control of AI, which causes tension. However, some Silicon Valley leaders believe collaboration is possible and needed. Working together could help solve big problems and create new opportunities for both nations. Despite current conflicts, AI cooperation might be a way to build better relations in the future.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **have different views** — 다른 견해를 갖다  
>   _People often **have different views** on politics._  
> - **work together** — 함께 일하다  
>   _Teams **work together** to finish projects faster._  
> - **cause tension** — 긴장을 초래하다  
>   _Disagreements can **cause tension** in relationships._  
> - **create new opportunities** — 새로운 기회를 만들다  
>   _The new policy will **create new opportunities** for workers._  
> - **build better relations** — 더 좋은 관계를 만들다  
>   _Countries try to **build better relations** through diplomacy._
