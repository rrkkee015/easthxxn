## 1. Trump’s Immigration Policy Sidelines Foreign Doctors Amid Shortage  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-10-1.png)  

The United States is facing a shortage of doctors, but new immigration policies under the Trump administration are making the problem worse. Foreign doctors, who often fill important roles in hospitals, find it harder to get work visas. This **job market** issue means fewer doctors can come to the US to help patients. Hospitals in smaller towns especially feel the impact because they rely heavily on these foreign doctors. Experts warn that this could lead to longer waits for medical care. The government says the policy protects American jobs, but critics argue it ignores the real needs of the healthcare system. How the US balances immigration rules and healthcare needs will be important in the coming years.

## 2. Job Market’s Effect on the Economy  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-10-2.png)  

The job market has a big effect on the US economy. When many people have jobs, they spend more money, which helps businesses grow. Right now, some places in the US have many job openings, but others are slower to hire. This difference can make the economy uneven. Experts watch the job market closely because it shows how healthy the economy is. If jobs disappear, people spend less, and the economy can slow down. On the other hand, a strong job market can lead to higher wages and more confidence. Understanding this balance helps leaders make good decisions.

> - **economy** — 경제

## 3. The U.S. and Silicon Valley May Be Running Out of Time to Deal with Taiwan  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-10-3.png)  

Taiwan is very important to the world’s technology industry. The United States and Silicon Valley companies depend on Taiwan’s factories to make computer chips. But there are growing political tensions with China, which claims Taiwan as its own. Experts warn the US may be running out of time to prepare for any problems that could affect this supply. If Taiwan faces conflict, it could hurt the global technology supply chain. Companies like Google and OpenAI are watching this situation closely. How the US works with Taiwan will shape the future of technology and international relations.

## 4. Trump Cautions Iran Against Charging Tolls Through Strait of Hormuz  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-10-4.png)  

Former President Trump recently warned Iran not to charge ships passing through the Strait of Hormuz. This narrow waterway is very important for global oil shipping. Iran has sometimes threatened to charge fees or block ships here. Trump said that the US will watch closely and act if needed to keep the passage open and safe. The area is tense because of ongoing conflicts and ceasefire talks. Many countries depend on this route for energy supplies. What happens here could affect world oil prices and international security.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **caution against** — ~하지 말라고 경고하다  
>   _The teacher **cautioned against** cheating on the test._  
> - **passage** — 통행로, 해협  
>   _The ship sailed through the narrow **passage** safely._  
> - **ceasefire** — 휴전  
>   _The two countries agreed to a **ceasefire** after long fighting._  
> - **act if needed** — 필요하면 행동하다  
>   _The police said they would **act if needed** to protect the public._
