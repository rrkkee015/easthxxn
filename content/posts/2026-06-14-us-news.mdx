## 1. Peace Deal Timeline Between US and Iran Faces Uncertainty 

The former US President Donald Trump said a peace deal with Iran would be signed on Sunday. However, officials from Iran quickly **disputed** this timeline, saying it might take longer. This deal is important because it could reduce tensions between the two countries and improve security in the region. The disagreement shows that peace talks are still difficult and not finished yet. Many experts are watching closely to see if both sides can agree soon. If the deal happens, it could change international relations and trade. But if talks fail, problems could continue or get worse.

## 2. Calls for Big Changes in US Immigration Before 2026 World Cup 

The United Nations human rights chief has asked the US to make a **massive rethink** of its immigration policies before the 2026 World Cup. Many players and fans from other countries will come to the US for this big soccer event. Current rules might make it hard for them to enter or stay in the country comfortably. The official said the US should prepare better to welcome visitors and protect their rights. This is important because the World Cup brings many people and money to the US. Changing immigration policies could help improve the country's global image. It might also affect other future international events.

## 3. US Job Market Remains Strong Despite Challenges 

The US job market is still strong, with many job openings in April. However, many workers feel **frustrated** because they have trouble finding good jobs or because prices for goods and services keep rising. This situation makes it harder for people to buy what they need. Some young people especially find it difficult to get summer jobs. Despite this, the number of open jobs shows that many companies want to hire workers. The economy faces challenges, but jobs are still available. Experts say the situation could change if prices keep going up or if international problems affect the economy.

## 4. Tech Industry Gains Influence in California Elections 

The technology industry spent millions of dollars in California's primary elections and won big. This means tech companies now have more power to influence laws and policies in the state. Many people worry that this could affect democracy because big money can control political decisions. Experts say we must not let wealthy business groups **buy off** our democracy. The tech industry’s role in politics is growing, and this could change how California handles issues like privacy, taxes, and labor laws. This story shows how money and technology are connected to politics today. People are watching to see if this influence will continue in future elections.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **buy off** — 매수하다, 매수하여 영향력을 행사하다  
>   _We cannot allow companies to **buy off** politicians._  
> - **influence** — 영향력  
>   _The tech industry has a strong **influence** on local laws._  
> - **primary elections** — 예비 선거  
>   _**Primary elections** decide which candidates will run in the main election._  
> - **democracy** — 민주주의  
>   _Protecting **democracy** means keeping elections fair and free._
