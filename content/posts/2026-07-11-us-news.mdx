## 1. New Housing Law to Pass Soon Despite President’s Objections  
  

Congress has agreed on a **bipartisan** housing bill that will become law in the next few hours. This means both Democrats and Republicans worked together to make this happen. The bill aims to improve affordable housing for many Americans. Even if former President Trump refuses to sign it, the law will still take effect because Congress has ways to approve laws without the president’s signature. This housing bill could help many families find better and cheaper homes. It is important because the U.S. faces a housing shortage and high rent prices. In the future, this law might lead to more government support for housing projects across the country.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **bipartisan** — 초당적인 (두 정당이 함께 하는)  
>   _The new law was **bipartisan**, with support from both major parties._  
> - **refuse to sign** — 서명하기를 거부하다  
>   _The president may **refuse to sign** the bill, but it can still pass._  
> - **take effect** — 발효되다, 효력이 생기다  
>   _The new policy will **take effect** next month._  
> - **housing shortage** — 주택 부족  
>   _Many cities have a serious **housing shortage**._

## 2. Controversy Over ICE Shooting in Houston  
  

A man was fatally shot by ICE officers in Houston, but the Department of Homeland Security said he was not the intended target. ICE is the agency responsible for immigration enforcement in the U.S. This shooting has raised serious questions about how immigration laws are enforced and the safety of people caught up in these operations. The government says they are reviewing the case carefully. Many people worry about the use of force by immigration officers. This event may lead to more calls for reform in immigration enforcement policies to protect people’s rights.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **fatally shot** — 치명상을 입고 총에 맞다  
>   _The suspect was **fatally shot** during the arrest._  
> - **not the intended target** — 원래 목표가 아니었던  
>   _The victim was **not the intended target** of the attack._  
> - **raise questions** — 의문을 제기하다  
>   _The incident **raised questions** about police conduct._  
> - **use of force** — 무력 사용  
>   _There is debate about the **use of force** by officers._

## 3. US Job Market Slows Down in June  
  

In June, many U.S. employers were **reluctant** to hire new workers. The number of new jobs added was lower than expected. This slowdown worries some experts because it may show that the economy is not growing as fast as before. However, the White House says the labor market is still on an **upward trajectory**, meaning it is slowly getting better. Hiring is important because it affects people’s incomes and spending. The situation will be watched closely in the coming months to see if the job market improves or worsens.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **reluctant** — 내키지 않는, 주저하는  
>   _Many companies are **reluctant** to hire during uncertain times._  
> - **slowdown** — 둔화, 경기 침체  
>   _There was a **slowdown** in job growth last month._  
> - **upward trajectory** — 상승 추세  
>   _The economy is on an **upward trajectory** despite recent challenges._  
> - **job market** — 취업 시장  
>   _The **job market** affects how easy it is to find work._

## 4. Tech Industry Faces Changes From Politics and AI  
  

The tech world is changing fast because of new political ideas and the rise of artificial intelligence (AI). Some people call this the rise of the “Silicon Valley Right,” which means more tech leaders support conservative politics. At the same time, companies must deal with new rules and shifting opinions about AI technology. President Trump’s changing statements about AI have confused many in the tech industry. In California, tech businesses spent millions of dollars in elections to protect their interests. These changes could affect how technology develops and how companies work with the government in the future.  

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **rise of** — ~의 등장, 부상  
>   _The **rise of** electric cars is changing the auto industry._  
> - **grapple with** — (~와) 씨름하다, 고심하다  
>   _The company is **grappling with** new AI regulations._  
> - **shift in opinion** — 의견 변화  
>   _There has been a **shift in opinion** about privacy online._  
> - **protect their interests** — 자신들의 이익을 보호하다  
>   _Tech firms spend money to **protect their interests** in politics._
