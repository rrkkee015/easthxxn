## 1. Senate Leaders Work to Prevent Government Shutdown ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-01-31-1.png)

Senate leaders are **scrambling** to reach a deal that would prevent a partial government shutdown. The government risks running out of money at midnight if a new funding agreement isn't reached. This situation often arises when there is a **disagreement** over budget priorities between political parties. In this case, both parties are trying to work together to find a solution that **satisfies** everyone. If they fail, many government services could be halted temporarily, affecting thousands of workers and citizens. The outcome of these negotiations is crucial as it impacts the daily lives of many Americans who rely on government services.

## 2. AI's Impact on Jobs: No Immediate Apocalypse ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-01-31-2.png)

The **introduction** of AI into the job market has raised concerns about massive job losses. However, a recent report suggests that the **apocalypse** isn't happening yet. AI is expected to change the nature of many jobs rather than completely replacing them in the immediate future. The technology can take over repetitive tasks, allowing workers to focus on more complex and creative aspects of their jobs. This shift means that while some jobs may be lost, new opportunities will also emerge. Businesses and workers need to adapt to these changes to stay competitive in the evolving job market.

> - **introduction** — 도입, 소개  
>   *The introduction of new technology has improved productivity.*
> - **apocalypse** — 대재앙, 종말  
>   *People feared an apocalypse when the comet was headed towards Earth.*
> - **shift** — 변화, 이동  
>   *There has been a shift in customer preferences towards online shopping.*

## 3. Trump's Public-Charge Rule and Immigrant Families ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-01-31-3.png)

The Trump administration proposed a **public-charge rule** that could have significant effects on immigrant families. This rule would make it harder for immigrants who use public benefits, like food stamps, to obtain green cards. The administration argues this will encourage self-sufficiency, but critics say it could **amplify** harm by forcing families to choose between necessary services and their immigration status. This policy reflects broader immigration strategies under the Trump administration, which have been the subject of much **debate**. The future of this rule is uncertain, as it faces legal challenges and could be changed by future administrations.

> 영어 표현 학습
> - **public-charge rule** — 공적부담 규정  
>   *The public-charge rule has been a controversial topic in immigration law.*
> - **amplify** — 증폭하다, 확대하다  
>   *The media can amplify a small issue into a major controversy.*
> - **debate** — 토론, 논쟁  
>   *There is a debate over the effectiveness of the new educational policy.*
