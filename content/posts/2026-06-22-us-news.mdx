## 1. JD Vance Begins Peace Talks with Iran Amid Rising Tensions 

JD Vance, a U.S. senator, has started talks with Iranian officials to reduce tensions between the two countries. This comes as former President Trump threatens military strikes over issues in the Hormuz Strait, a key shipping route. The talks aim to find peaceful solutions and avoid conflict. These discussions are important because they could help prevent war in a very sensitive region. If successful, peace talks might lead to better relations and more stability in the Middle East. However, the situation is still uncertain, and both sides need to agree to work together. Many hope these talks will **break the ice** and open doors for future cooperation.

## 3. Many Employers Post Jobs but Are Not Actually Hiring 

Many companies in the U.S. are posting job ads, but they are not hiring as many workers as it seems. Experts say this happens because businesses want to be ready when the economy improves, or they want to keep options open. This means job seekers might feel confused about the real chances of getting hired. The job market looks busy, but there is still a lot of **labor market slack**, meaning not all workers are hired yet. This situation might continue for some time, making it harder for people who need jobs now. Understanding this can help job seekers be more patient and careful in their search.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **posting jobs** — 일자리를 게시하다  
>   _Many companies are **posting jobs** even if they do not hire immediately._  
> - **not actually hiring** — 실제로 고용하지 않다  
>   _Some employers are **not actually hiring**, just preparing._  
> - **labor market slack** — 노동 시장의 여유분  
>   _There is still **labor market slack** despite many job ads._  
> - **keep options open** — 선택지를 열어두다  
>   _Employers want to **keep options open** for future hiring._  
> - **job seekers** — 구직자들  
>   _**Job seekers** should be aware of the real hiring situation._

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Influence Faces New Challenges in Europe 

Europe is starting to challenge the power of U.S. big tech companies, but it still follows many Silicon Valley ideas. This shows that even though countries want to control technology, they often use the same rules and methods from the U.S. tech world. The article talks about how Europe wants more control but finds it hard to create different systems. This situation is important because tech companies affect many parts of life and business around the globe. The future may bring changes in how tech is managed, with new rules and competition. Watching these changes can help us understand how technology will grow worldwide.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **challenge the power** — 권력에 도전하다  
>   _Europe wants to **challenge the power** of big tech companies._  
> - **big tech companies** — 대형 기술 회사들  
>   _**Big tech companies** have large influence globally._  
> - **follow ideas** — 아이디어를 따르다  
>   _Many countries **follow ideas** from Silicon Valley._  
> - **create different systems** — 다른 시스템을 만들다  
>   _Europe tries to **create different systems** for tech regulation._  
> - **affect many parts** — 많은 부분에 영향을 미치다  
>   _Technology **affects many parts** of our daily lives._
