## 1. Rising Tensions: U.S. Nears Possible War with Iran 

The United States is facing a serious situation with Iran after more American **troops** were killed recently. This event has many people worried that the U.S. might return to an **all-out war** with Iran. An all-out war means a full, open fight with many battles and serious consequences. The situation started after years of conflict and problems between the two countries. The U.S. government is now deciding how to respond carefully to avoid **escalating** the conflict. This news is important because it affects not only these countries but also the whole world’s safety and oil markets. People will watch closely to see if the U.S. sends more troops or tries to find a peaceful solution.

## 2. U.S. Immigration Policy Talk at University of Wisconsin 

Doris Meissner, an expert on immigration, recently gave a talk at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee about U.S. immigration policy. She explained how immigration rules have changed over time and why they are important for the country’s future. The U.S. has many complicated laws that affect different groups, like students, workers, and families. Meissner said it is important to find a balance between security and welcoming new people. This talk helps students and the public understand the challenges and opportunities in U.S. immigration. Learning about these policies is useful because immigration affects jobs, culture, and the economy.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **immigration policy** — 이민 정책  
>   _The government changed its **immigration policy** to allow more skilled workers._  
> - **balance between** — ~사이의 균형  
>   _We need a **balance between** safety and freedom._  
> - **opportunities** — 기회들  
>   _Immigration creates new **opportunities** for people and businesses._

## 3. Hiring in the U.S. Slows Down But Remains Steady 

The job market in the U.S. is changing. Hiring is still happening, but at a **slower pace** than before. Many companies are being careful about how many new workers they bring in. This is partly because the economy is uncertain and some industries face challenges. Experts say the slower hiring means workers may find it harder to get new jobs soon. However, the job market is not collapsing; it is just moving more slowly than earlier this year. For many people, this means they should keep learning new skills to stay **competitive** when looking for work. The future might bring more opportunities if the economy improves.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **slower pace** — 더 느린 속도  
>   _The company is hiring at a **slower pace** because of budget limits._  
> - **job market** — 일자리를 찾는 시장  
>   _The **job market** is tough for new graduates this year._  
> - **competitive** — 경쟁이 치열한  
>   _You need to be **competitive** to get a good job in this city._

## 4. New AI Model from China Challenges U.S. Tech Leaders 

A new artificial intelligence (AI) model called Kimi K3 from China is causing a big reaction in Silicon Valley. This AI is designed to compete with popular models like ChatGPT and Claude. Many experts believe this shows how global tech companies are racing to create better AI tools. The competition is strong because AI can change how people work, learn, and communicate. Kimi K3’s arrival means U.S. companies must improve quickly to stay **ahead**. This story is important because AI is becoming a big part of the economy and daily life worldwide.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **artificial intelligence (AI)** — 인공지능  
>   _Many companies use **artificial intelligence** to improve their products._  
> - **compete with** — ~와 경쟁하다  
>   _This phone **competes with** other brands in the market._  
> - **stay ahead** — 앞서 나가다, 경쟁에서 우위에 서다  
>   _To **stay ahead**, the company invests in new technologies._
