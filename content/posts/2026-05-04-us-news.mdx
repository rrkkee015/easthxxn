## 1. New Immigration Dashboard Shows USCIS Backlogs and Trends  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-05-04-1.png)

The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) has released a new online dashboard. This tool gives important information about **backlogs** and how long it takes to process applications. Many immigrants wait a long time to get decisions on their cases. The dashboard helps people see where delays happen and which types of cases take longer. This is useful for immigrants, lawyers, and policymakers. It may also help improve the immigration system by making it more **transparent** and easier to understand. In the future, this could lead to faster processing and better service for applicants.

## 2. U.S. Jobless Claims Drop to Lowest in Over 50 Years  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-05-04-2.png)

The number of people applying for unemployment benefits in the U.S. has fallen to 189,000. This is the lowest level in more than five decades. It means more people are keeping their jobs or finding new ones. The strong job market shows the economy is doing well right now. However, some experts say not all Americans feel this success equally. Many people in smaller cities and rural areas do not see the same job growth. Still, the overall low **jobless claims** number is a positive sign for workers and businesses. It may lead to more confidence in the economy and consumer spending.

## 3. Silicon Valley’s New Political Shift Changes Tech Landscape  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-05-04-3.png)

Silicon Valley is well known as a center for technology and innovation. Recently, a new political group called the “Silicon Valley Right” is gaining influence. This group supports more conservative ideas than the traditional tech leaders. They want changes in policies about technology, business, and society. This shift could affect how companies operate and what products they create. It also shows how politics is becoming more important in the tech world. Experts say this may lead to more debates inside Silicon Valley and change its culture over time.

## 4. Pellet Found in Secret Service Agent’s Vest Links to White House Attack  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-05-04-4.png)

A recent investigation found a pellet inside a Secret Service agent’s protective vest. This pellet is connected to a suspect involved in an attack near the White House Correspondents’ Dinner. The discovery is important because it helps link the suspect to the crime. The Secret Service protects the president and other officials, so this case is serious. Authorities are continuing to gather evidence and learn more about the attack. This incident raises concerns about security near important government events. It might lead to stronger safety measures in the future.
