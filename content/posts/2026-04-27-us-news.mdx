The shooting caused fear and confusion, but Trump explained he stayed calm and focused on the event. This news is important because it shows how people react in emergencies, especially leaders. It also reminds us about the safety needed at big public events. Security teams are reviewing their plans to prevent future problems. Many people are curious about how politicians handle these stressful moments. The story helps us understand the importance of staying calm in difficult situations.

## 2. New Immigration Policies and Actions This Week ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-27-2.png)

This week in immigration news, the Justice Department started a new effort to **target hundreds** of people who became U.S. citizens but may have lied during their application. This process is called denaturalization. It means they could lose their citizenship if officials find problems in their history. At the same time, there are many updates about immigration rules that affect people waiting to enter or stay in the U.S. These changes are important because they impact many families and workers across the country. Some groups worry these actions may be too strict. Watching how these policies change helps us understand the future of immigration in America.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **target hundreds** — 수백 명을 대상으로 하다  
>   _The campaign will **target hundreds** of young voters._  
> - **denaturalization** — 시민권 박탈  
>   _The government can start **denaturalization** if someone lied on their application._  
> - **impact many** — 많은 사람에게 영향을 미치다  
>   _The new law will **impact many** families in the city._  
> - **be too strict** — 너무 엄격하다

## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Signs of Slowing Down ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-27-3.png)

Recent reports show that hiring in the U.S. has **dropped to pandemic lows**. This means fewer companies are offering new jobs now compared to recent years. Many experts say the job market is not growing as fast as before. This slowdown can affect workers looking for new opportunities. Some blame changes in government policies for the slower hiring. Others think the economy is just going through a natural pause. Understanding these changes helps workers prepare for what may come next. It also shows how important the job market is for everyday life.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **dropped to pandemic lows** — 팬데믹 기간 중 최저치로 떨어졌다  
>   _The number of tourists **dropped to pandemic lows** last month._  
> - **job market** — 일자리 시장  
>   _The **job market** is competitive for new graduates._  
> - **look for opportunities** — 기회를 찾다  
>   _She moved to the city to **look for opportunities** in her field._  
> - **natural pause** — 자연스러운 일시 정지  
>   _The company took a **natural pause** in hiring during the winter._

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces Challenges as Tech Workers Struggle ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-27-4.png)

Silicon Valley is famous as the tech hub of the United States. But recently, many of the most **sought-after workers** have become **unemployable**. This means they are having a hard time finding jobs, even though they used to be in high demand. Experts say this is because of changes in the tech industry and some companies cutting jobs. The story shows that even in strong markets, workers can face uncertainty. It also points to how fast technology and business needs can change. Watching Silicon Valley helps us learn about the future of work and technology in America.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **sought-after workers** — 수요가 많은 노동자들  
>   _She is one of the most **sought-after workers** in her field._  
> - **unemployable** — 일자리를 구할 수 없는  
>   _After the company closed, many workers became **unemployable**._  
> - **cut jobs** — 일자리를 줄이다, 해고하다  
>   _The company had to **cut jobs** because of lower sales._  
> - **face uncertainty** — 불확실성에 직면하다  
>   _Many small businesses **face uncertainty** during the economic crisis._
