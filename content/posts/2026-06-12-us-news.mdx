## 1. Trump Cancels Iran Strike Plans, Says a Deal Is Near  
  

Former President Donald Trump announced that he has **canceled** plans to strike Iran. He said this is because a **deal is near** between the United States and Iran. Trump did not give many details about the deal, but his decision is a big change from earlier threats. This news could help reduce tensions between the two countries. If the deal works out, it might lead to better relations and less chance of conflict. Many people are watching closely to see what happens next. This story is important because it shows how quickly politics can change.

## 2. US Immigration Policy Creates Problems for World Cup Visitors  
  

As the World Cup soccer event starts, some visitors have had trouble entering the United States because of strict immigration policies. Many fans and players come from different countries, but some were **turned away** at the border. This has caused disappointment and frustration. The United Nations human rights chief has asked the US to **rethink** its immigration rules to make them more welcoming. The World Cup is a time for people to come together, but immigration problems are creating a **chilling effect**. This means visitors may feel unwelcome or afraid to travel to the US. How the government changes these policies could affect future international events.
