## 1. US Responds Quickly to Iran Missile Attack 

Recently, Iran launched a missile attack that killed two U.S. troops and left one missing. This act increased tensions between the two countries. The U.S. decided to **swiftly punish** Iran by launching new airstrikes. The goal is to show that attacks on American forces will not be tolerated. This situation is serious because it could lead to more conflict in the Middle East. Many people are watching closely to see what happens next. The U.S. government says it wants to protect its troops and keep peace in the region.

## 2. New U.S. Immigration Rules Target Students and Spouses 

The U.S. government recently introduced new rules that make it harder for some immigrants to stay legally. These rules mainly **target students** and spouses of U.S. citizens. The new laws mean that some people who want to study or live with their families could face more challenges. Supporters say the rules protect American jobs, but critics worry they are too strict. This change affects many families and could change the future of immigration in the U.S. Experts suggest watching how these rules are applied over time.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **target students** — 학생들을 겨냥하다  
>   _The new policy **targets students** from certain countries._  
> - **face challenges** — 어려움에 직면하다  
>   _New immigrants often **face challenges** when adjusting to a new culture._  
> - **protect jobs** — 일자리를 보호하다  
>   _The law aims to **protect jobs** for local workers._  
> - **over time** — 시간이 흐르면서  
>   _The effects of the policy will become clearer **over time**._

## 3. Job Growth Slows, Leaving Many Workers Locked Out 

The U.S. job market showed slow growth recently, with only 57,000 new jobs added in June. This is much less than expected. Experts say about two million workers are still **locked out** of the improving job market. This means many people want jobs but cannot find them, even though the economy is getting better. Some reasons include a shift in job types and skill requirements. This situation is important because it affects many families and the overall economy. Workers are hoping for more opportunities soon.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **job growth slows** — 일자리 증가가 둔화되다  
>   _After a strong start, **job growth slows** in the last quarter._  
> - **locked out** — 배제되다, 진입하지 못하다  
>   _Many young people feel **locked out** of the housing market._  
> - **shift in job types** — 직종의 변화  
>   _There is a **shift in job types** due to new technology._  
> - **affect families** — 가족에게 영향을 미치다  
>   _Economic changes **affect families** across the country._

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces New Competition in AI Technology 

Silicon Valley, known for leading in technology, is facing strong competition in the field of artificial intelligence (AI). New AI models from other countries, like China’s Kimi K3, are **sending shockwaves** through the area. These models challenge popular AI like ChatGPT and Claude. The competition pushes companies to improve their technology faster. AI is important because it affects many parts of daily life and business. Experts say this rivalry will continue to grow and change the tech world.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **sending shockwaves** — 충격을 주다, 큰 반향을 일으키다  
>   _The new product is **sending shockwaves** through the industry._  
> - **challenge popular** — 인기 있는 것에 도전하다  
>   _The startup wants to **challenge popular** brands._  
> - **push companies to improve** — 기업들이 개선하도록 압박하다  
>   _Competition **pushes companies to improve** their services._  
> - **continue to grow** — 계속 성장하다  
>   _The market for electric cars will **continue to grow**._
