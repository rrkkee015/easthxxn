## 1. Long Security Lines at Houston and New Orleans Airports ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-09-1.png)

Many travelers in Houston and New Orleans are facing **long security queues** at airports. This means people have to wait a lot of time to get through security checks before their flights. The delays are caused by more passengers traveling during busy times and fewer security staff available. These long waits can make people miss their flights or feel stressed before traveling. Airports and security agencies are working to fix the problem by adding more workers and improving processes. This issue is important because air travel is a big part of daily life, especially for business and family trips. If the problem gets better, passengers will have a smoother experience at the airport.

> - **long security queues** — 긴 보안 대기줄  
>   _We had to stand in a **long security queue** at the airport._  
> - **caused by** — ~에 의해 야기된, 원인이 된  
>   _The delay was **caused by** heavy traffic._  
> - **miss their flights** — 비행기를 놓치다  
>   _If the line is too slow, some people might **miss their flights**._  
> - **working to fix** — 고치려고 노력하는 중이다  
>   _The airport is **working to fix** the problems quickly._

## 2. Immigrants Help Make the Labor Market Strong ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-09-2.png)

A recent report shows that immigrants play an important role in the U.S. labor market. They help keep the economy growing by filling jobs in many industries. Immigrants often take jobs that are hard to fill, which helps businesses stay open and serve customers. Their work also supports innovation and new ideas. The report says that without immigrants, the job market would be weaker and less flexible. This story helps us understand why immigration is a big part of American life and the economy. It also shows why debates about immigration affect not just people but jobs and businesses too.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **play an important role** — 중요한 역할을 하다  
>   _Immigrants **play an important role** in many industries._  
> - **keep the economy growing** — 경제 성장을 유지하다  
>   _Their work helps **keep the economy growing**._  
> - **fill jobs** — 일자리를 채우다  
>   _Immigrants **fill jobs** that are hard to find workers for._  
> - **job market** — 노동 시장, 일자리 시장

## 3. U.S. Job Growth Fell Sharply in February ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-09-3.png)

In February, the U.S. added fewer new jobs than expected, and the unemployment rate rose to 4.4%. This means fewer people found new work, and more people were looking for jobs. Experts say this change shows the job market is **changing quickly** and could affect the economy soon. Some industries hired fewer workers, while others stayed the same or grew slowly. The job market is important because it shows how healthy the economy is and how easy it is for people to find work. Policymakers will watch these numbers to decide if they need to change economic rules or support workers.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **job growth fell** — 일자리 증가가 감소했다  
>   _Last month, **job growth fell** unexpectedly._  
> - **unemployment rate** — 실업률  
>   _The **unemployment rate** went up to 4.4%._  
> - **changing quickly** — 빠르게 변하고 있다  
>   _The job market is **changing quickly** these days._  
> - **affect the economy** — 경제에 영향을 미치다  
>   _Job numbers can **affect the economy** in many ways._

## 4. Silicon Valley Faces a Big Risk with Taiwan Chip Supply ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-09-4.png)

Silicon Valley depends heavily on computer chips made in Taiwan. These chips are important for many devices like smartphones, cars, and computers. But now, there is a growing worry about a **looming disaster** if Taiwan’s chip factories stop working. Political tensions and natural risks could disrupt the supply. If this happens, it would hurt many American tech companies and slow down technology progress. Experts say the U.S. should prepare by finding new ways to make chips and reduce this risk. This story shows how global connections affect local businesses and why planning ahead is important.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **looming disaster** — 임박한 재앙, 다가오는 큰 문제  
>   _Experts warn about a **looming disaster** in chip supply._  
> - **depends heavily on** — ~에 크게 의존하다  
>   _Silicon Valley **depends heavily on** Taiwan chips._  
> - **disrupt the supply** — 공급을 방해하다  
>   _Tensions could **disrupt the supply** of important parts._  
> - **planning ahead** — 미리 계획하기  
>   _Good companies focus on **planning ahead** to avoid problems._
