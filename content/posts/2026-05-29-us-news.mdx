## 1. US and Iran Try to Keep a Fragile Truce Alive 

The United States and Iran are trying to **extend** a truce, but it is still **TBD** (to be decided) if former President Trump will agree to a new deal. Both sides want to avoid conflict, especially with tensions high in the Middle East. This truce is important because it helps keep peace and may affect global oil prices and security. The current talks are slow, and many people are watching closely to see if a longer peace deal is possible. If the deal is not signed, there could be more **uncertainty** in the region. For now, the situation remains unclear, and both countries are waiting for the next steps.

## 2. New USCIS Memo Could Change How You Apply for a Green Card 

A new memo from the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) may **force** most green card applicants to apply from outside the U.S. This change could cause a lot of **confusion** and **chaos** for many people who want to live and work in America. Usually, applicants can apply from inside the country, but this new rule may make it harder and more complicated. Experts worry this will **delay** many cases and increase stress for families. At the same time, the government says the rule is needed to keep the process fair and secure. We will have to wait and see if this change goes into effect and how it affects immigrants.

> - **force** — 강요하다  
>   _The new law will **force** businesses to change their policies._  
> - **confusion** — 혼란  
>   _There was a lot of **confusion** about the new schedule._  
> - **chaos** — 대혼란  
>   _The sudden change caused **chaos** in the airport._  
> - **delay** — 지연시키다

## 3. Job Market Shows Strength Despite Global Uncertainty 

In April, U.S. employers added a strong 115,000 jobs, and unemployment stayed low. This is a good sign for the economy, especially with worries about war and global problems. Even though jobless claims rose a bit to 215,000, layoffs remain low, meaning companies are still hiring and keeping workers. Experts say a healthy **job market** helps people feel more secure and supports economic growth. However, some uncertainty remains because of international issues like the Iran conflict. Overall, the job market’s strength is encouraging for workers and businesses.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Rapid Rise May Be Slowing Down 

Silicon Valley, known as the heart of American technology, grew very fast and changed the Bay Area. But now, some experts say its **meteoric rise** might be coming to an end. Many big tech companies are changing, and new tech hubs are starting to appear in other parts of the country. This could mean Silicon Valley will not be the only place for tech innovation anymore. The region still has many strengths, but it faces challenges like rising costs and competition. People are watching to see what the future holds for this famous tech center.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **meteoric rise** — 급격한 상승

> - **heart of** — ~의 중심

> - **tech hub** — 기술 중심지
