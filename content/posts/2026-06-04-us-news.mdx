## 1. US House Pushes Back on Iran War Powers 

The US House of Representatives passed a resolution to **limit** President Trump's power to wage war against Iran. This is a rare example of Congress **pushing back** against the president's military actions. The resolution asks the president to get approval from Congress before taking further military steps in Iran. This move came after rising tensions between the US and Iran that worried many lawmakers. It shows Congress wants to have more control over important decisions about war. Some experts say this could lead to more debates about the president’s authority in foreign policy. The resolution is a sign of growing concern about the risks of conflict in the Middle East. If the Senate agrees, it could change how the US handles military power in the future.

## 2. New Green Card Rules May Change Immigrants’ Lives 

The US government announced **new rules** that will change how immigrants get green cards. These changes could make it harder or easier for some people to stay in the US legally. The new rules focus on speeding up the process but also include stricter requirements. Some immigrants worry they will face more delays or higher costs. The government says the goal is to make immigration fair and efficient. These rules could affect many families and workers who want to live and work in the US. Experts say immigrants should learn about these changes soon to prepare. The new rules may also influence the US economy and communities.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **new rules** — 새로운 규칙  
>   _The government introduced **new rules** for green card applicants._  
> - **speed up** — 빠르게 하다, 속도를 높이다  
>   _They want to **speed up** the immigration process._  
> - **stricter** — 더 엄격한  
>   _The rules are **stricter** for some types of applications._  
> - **face delays** — 지연을 겪다  
>   _Many immigrants may **face delays** in getting their green cards._  
> - **prepare** — 준비하다  
>   _It is important to **prepare** for the new immigration rules._

## 3. US Job Openings Rise Despite Economic Concerns 

In April, the number of job openings in the US rose to 7.6 million, even though there are worries about the economy because of tensions with Iran. This means many companies want to hire workers, but the number of people actually getting hired did not grow as fast. Experts say the job market remains **strong** but uncertain. Some businesses are cautious about adding new staff because of global problems. The rise in job openings shows that many employers still need workers, especially in private companies. This could be good news for people looking for jobs, but there are challenges ahead. The government will watch job data closely to understand the economy’s health.

## 4. Indian Engineers Question Moving to Silicon Valley 

Many Indian engineers once dreamed of working in Silicon Valley, the US technology hub. But now, some say, "I would not come to the US" because of changes in work culture and immigration. They spent years chasing job opportunities, but long work hours and visa problems make it hard. The US tech industry is also facing strong competition from China, where workers often work 12-hour days. Some engineers feel they can find better balance and chances at home or in other countries. This shift could change the future of Silicon Valley and how global tech talent moves. It shows how important work-life balance and immigration policies are for attracting skilled workers.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **dream of** — ~을 꿈꾸다  
>   _Many young people **dream of** working in Silicon Valley._  
> - **work culture** — 직장 문화  
>   _The **work culture** in tech can be very demanding._  
> - **chase opportunities** — 기회를 쫓다  
>   _Engineers **chase opportunities** in different countries._  
> - **work-life balance** — 일과 삶의 균형  
>   _Good **work-life balance** is important for happiness._  
> - **skilled workers** — 숙련 노동자  
>   _The US wants to attract more **skilled workers**._
