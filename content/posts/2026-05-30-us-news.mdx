## 1. Trump’s Name Removed from Kennedy Center Building 

A judge has ordered the Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C., to **remove** former President Donald Trump’s name from one of its buildings. This decision is important because the Kennedy Center is a famous place for the arts and culture. The name was added during Trump’s presidency, but now the court says it should be taken off. This change shows how public places can reflect political opinions and history. The Kennedy Center will have to decide what new name to use next. This story shows how names on buildings can matter a lot to many people. We may see more decisions like this in the future about other public places.

## 2. New USCIS Memo Could Change How Green Card Applicants Apply 

The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) released a new memo that may **force** most green card applicants to apply from outside the United States. This change could cause a lot of confusion and problems for people waiting for their green cards. Usually, many applicants apply while they are in the U.S., but now they might have to leave the country first. This new rule may slow down the process and make it harder for families to stay together. Immigration groups are worried about the impact on people’s lives. It is not clear when or how this rule will start, but it will affect many immigrants.

## 3. Job Market Trends Show Mixed Signals for the Economy 

The U.S. job market is sending mixed messages about the economy right now. Jobless claims recently **rose** to 215,000, but layoffs are still low. This means that although more people are applying for unemployment benefits, companies are not firing many workers. Experts say this is because of uncertainty from events like the conflict in Iran. Some industries are cautious about hiring new workers, while others are keeping jobs stable. The job market affects the whole economy because people's incomes and spending depend on having work. It will be important to watch these trends in the coming months.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Rise Faces New Challenges 

Silicon Valley has been known for its **meteoric** rise, shaping the Bay Area and the tech world. But now, experts wonder if this rapid growth is coming to an end. The area grew very fast because of startups and big tech companies that created many jobs and wealth. However, recent changes like high housing costs and shifting work habits may slow down this growth. Some engineers and workers feel uncertain about the future. This story shows how even very successful regions can face challenges that change their path.

> ### 영어 표현 학습

>   _Her career had a **meteoric rise** after winning the competition._  
> - **shape** — 형성하다  
>   _New policies will **shape** the future of education._  
> - **challenge** — 도전, 어려움  
>   _The company faces many **challenges** in the global market._  
> - **uncertain** — 불확실한
