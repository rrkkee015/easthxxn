## 1. US Strikes Over 80 Iran Targets and Reimposes Sanctions 

The United States recently **hit** more than 80 targets in Iran as part of a military action. This happened after tensions between the two countries increased. The US also **reimposed sanctions** on Iran, which means they put back economic rules to limit Iran’s trade and money flow. This move is part of a long conflict that affects world politics and oil prices. Experts say these actions could make the situation worse or force Iran to change its policies. Many people watch closely to see how other countries will react. This is important because it could affect global peace and the economy.

## 3. US Hiring Slows Down in June After Strong Growth 

The US job market showed a big change in June. After many months of fast job growth, only 57,000 new jobs were added in June. This number is much lower than before. Experts say employers are now more **reluctant** to hire many workers. This could be because of economic worries or higher interest rates. A slow job market might affect how people spend money and the whole economy. Many people watch these numbers to understand the future of work and money.

At the same time, China is investing a lot of money and resources into AI. This race is not just about business; it also affects national security and global influence. Experts say this **AI war** will shape how technology develops worldwide.
