## 1. Supreme Court Limits a Key Part of the Voting Rights Act  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-30-1.png)

The Supreme Court made a big decision about the Voting Rights Act. This law was created to protect people's right to vote, especially in places with a history of discrimination. The Court **limited** an important part of the law that helped prevent unfair voting rules. This means some states might change their voting rules without federal approval. Many people worry this could make it harder for some groups to vote. Voting rights are very important for democracy, so this decision could affect future elections. Experts say we need to watch how states change their voting laws next.

## 2. Trump’s Immigration Limits May Slow U.S. Population Growth  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-30-2.png)

The U.S. government might change immigration rules that could **sharply reduce** how many people come to live in the country. These new limits would affect legal immigration, which is how many people enter the U.S. with permission. If fewer immigrants arrive, the U.S. population may grow more slowly. This is important because immigrants help the economy and communities grow. Some people support the limits to control immigration, but others worry about the effects on jobs and culture. Watching these rule changes helps us understand the future of America’s population.

## 3. U.S. Job Market Shows Strong Growth in March  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-30-3.png)

In March, U.S. employers **added** 178,000 jobs, which was more than many experts expected. This shows the job market is still strong, even after some slow months. More jobs mean people can find work and earn money. However, some experts say this hiring increase may not last long. The economy faces challenges like inflation and global problems that could slow growth. Still, this report gives hope to workers looking for jobs or better pay. The job market is important because it affects everyday life for millions of people.

## 4. Silicon Valley’s Political Shift: Why Tech Is Turning Conservative  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-30-4.png)

Silicon Valley, known for being very liberal, is seeing some important changes. More tech workers and leaders are becoming **conservative** or right-leaning. This shift is surprising because tech companies have usually supported liberal ideas. Some say this change is about free speech and new ideas in technology. Others think economic or cultural reasons are behind this. This change could affect how tech companies work and what policies they support. Watching Silicon Valley's political shift helps us understand the future of the tech industry.
