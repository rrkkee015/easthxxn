## 1. US-Iran Oil Deal Offers Hope but Leaves Problems Unsolved  


Recently, the US and Iran made a deal that might allow oil to flow again. This is important because oil affects the world’s economy and energy prices. However, the deal does not fix the **root problems** in the Middle East. These problems include political conflicts and mistrust between countries. Many experts say the deal is only a first step, not a complete solution. The region still faces challenges that could cause more tension in the future. People are watching closely to see if this deal will bring real peace or just temporary relief.

## 2. US Immigration Policies Hurt Prenatal Care and Families  


New US immigration policies are causing problems for pregnant women and families. These policies **interfere with prenatal care**, which is the health care a mother gets before the baby is born. Because of the rules, some people avoid going to the doctor or hospitals. This hurts their health and the health of their babies. Communities are also affected because families feel stressed and uncertain. Experts say the government should think about how policies affect real people’s lives. Better support could help families stay healthy and safe.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **interfere with** — ~을 방해하다  
>   _Loud noises **interfere with** my concentration._  
> - **prenatal care** — 산전 관리  
>   _Good **prenatal care** helps ensure a healthy baby._  
> - **avoid** — 피하다  
>   _She tries to **avoid** eating too much sugar._  
> - **stress and uncertain** — 스트레스와 불확실성  
>   _Job loss can cause **stress and uncertain** about the future._

## 3. Why Are Many People Struggling to Find Jobs Despite Growth?  


The US economy is adding jobs every month, but many people still have trouble finding work. This is confusing because more jobs usually mean more chances for workers. Experts say the **job market** is changing. Some jobs require new skills that many people don’t have yet. Also, some people want different kinds of work or better pay. This gap between jobs and workers causes frustration. Understanding these problems can help governments and companies create better job programs. The situation may improve if training and job matching get better.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **job market** — 구직 시장, 노동 시장  
>   _The **job market** can be very competitive for new graduates._  
> - **adding jobs** — 일자리를 늘리다  
>   _The company is **adding jobs** to handle more customers._  
> - **gap** — 차이, 격차  
>   _There is a **gap** between supply and demand for housing._  
> - **frustration** — 좌절감  
>   _Losing a game caused him great **frustration**._

## 4. Europe Starts to Question US Tech Power but Follows Its Rules  


Europe is beginning to **break up with** big US tech companies. This means European countries want more control over their own technology. They are worried about privacy and too much power in the hands of American companies. However, Europe still follows many rules set by Silicon Valley, the US tech center. This shows it is hard to make big changes quickly. The tech world is watching to see if Europe will create new paths or stay connected to US tech. This could affect how technology works worldwide in the future.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **break up with** — ~와 결별하다, 헤어지다  
>   _She decided to **break up with** her old habits._  
> - **follow rules** — 규칙을 따르다  
>   _All players must **follow rules** during the game._  
> - **too much power** — 너무 많은 권력  
>   _The leader had **too much power** and made unfair decisions._  
> - **tech world** — 기술 세계, IT 업계  
>   _The **tech world** is always changing quickly._
