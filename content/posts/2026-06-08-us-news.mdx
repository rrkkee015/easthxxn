## 1. Stabbing at New York's Penn Station Shocks Commuters 

Early this week, six people were hurt in a stabbing attack at Penn Station in New York City. Police caught the suspect quickly and took him into custody. Penn Station is one of the busiest train stations in the US, so this attack caused a lot of fear and confusion. Authorities are still investigating why the suspect attacked people. This event reminds everyone how important it is to stay alert in crowded places. The police are increasing security to make sure travelers feel safe again. Penn Station has been a key part of New York's transportation for many years, so this incident is very serious.

## 2. Federal Court Orders Restart of US Immigration Processing 

A federal judge recently ruled that the US government must restart asylum and immigration processing. This decision affects many people from 39 countries who had faced new immigration restrictions. The judge said the government’s rules caused chaos and **confusion** for immigrants trying to enter the US legally. Many workers, especially from the Philippines, have been worried because the immigration policies keep changing. Immigration is a complex and important issue in the US, and this ruling could help thousands who want to live and work here. The government will now have to follow the court’s order and improve the immigration process.

## 3. US Adds 172,000 Jobs in Hospitality and Service Sectors 

The US economy added 172,000 new jobs last month, according to a recent report. Many of these jobs are in restaurants, bars, and hotels. This shows that people are spending more on travel and dining as the economy improves. While the job growth is slower than before, it is still steady and helps many workers find employment. Experts say that the job market is recovering after difficult times during the pandemic. These new jobs are important because they give opportunities to young people and those who lost work earlier. The government will continue to watch how the job market changes in the coming months.

## 4. Many Indian Tech Workers Are Leaving Silicon Valley 

A surprising number of Indian tech workers left the US last year, especially from Silicon Valley. Around 15,000 returned to India, and more are expected to follow. Many say they are leaving because of visa problems, high living costs, and a feeling that the US is less welcoming now. Silicon Valley has long been a dream place for engineers from around the world, but this trend shows changes in the tech industry and immigration. Some workers say they would not recommend coming to the US anymore. This shift could affect the future of tech companies and the US economy.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **leave a place** — 어떤 장소를 떠나다  
>   _Many workers **left** the city after the factory closed._  
> - **visa problems** — 비자 문제  
>   _**Visa problems** make it hard for people to stay in the country._  
> - **high living costs** — 높은 생활비  
>   _**High living costs** are a reason some people move to smaller cities._  
> - **less welcoming** — 환영하지 않는 분위기  
>   _Some immigrants feel the community is **less welcoming** than before._  
> - **affect the future** — 미래에 영향을 미치다  
>   _These changes could **affect the future** of the company._
