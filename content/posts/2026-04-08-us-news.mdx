## 1. US and Iran Start Ceasefire Talks to Avoid War ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-08-1.png)

The United States and Iran have agreed to a **ceasefire**, which means they will stop fighting for now. This deal starts a two-week period of talks to find a longer solution. The agreement delays a possible large attack by the US, which was once threatened by former President Trump. Both countries want to avoid a war that could cause many problems. This is important because fighting between them could affect many other countries. Diplomats will work hard to keep peace during these talks. If successful, this could help calm tensions in the Middle East and protect many lives.

## 2. Tennessee Passes New Immigration Bill Amid Debate ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-08-2.png)

The Tennessee Senate recently passed a new immigration policy bill that many people find **controversial**. The bill includes strict rules about immigrants living and working in the state. Supporters say the bill will improve safety and protect jobs for local people. However, critics worry it might hurt immigrant families and cause unfair treatment. Immigration is a hot topic in many US states right now. This bill could have big effects on communities in Tennessee and might influence other states. The debate about immigration laws will likely continue for some time.

## 3. US Job Market Shows Strong Growth Despite Global Concerns ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-08-3.png)

The US labor market is doing well, with the largest job gains in 15 months. In March, employers added 178,000 new jobs, showing that many companies are hiring again. This is good news for workers looking for jobs. However, there are still worries because of the conflict between the US and Iran. Some experts think this tension could slow down job growth in the future. For now, the job market remains strong and helps support the economy. People will watch closely to see how global events affect jobs in the coming months.
