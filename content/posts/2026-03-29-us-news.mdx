## 1. Protests Against Trump’s Agenda Grow Across the US  
![Article 1](/images/news/2026-03-29-1.png)

Many people in the US are holding protests called “No Kings” to show they do not support former President Trump and his political plans. These protests have happened in several cities. The people involved say they want to stop Trump from having too much power. They believe his ideas could hurt democracy and fairness. These protests are important because they show how divided the country still is. Some experts think these events will continue as the next elections get closer. It is clear that many Americans are ready to **take action** and express their opinions in public.

## 2. Immigration Funding Faces Uncertainty in Congress  
![Article 2](/images/news/2026-03-29-2.png)

The funding for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), which handles immigration policies, is still uncertain. Recently, a disagreement among Republicans stopped a compromise that could have provided money for DHS. This means immigration policies might stay unclear for a longer time. Many people worry this will cause problems at the border and affect immigrants waiting for help. The issue is important because DHS is responsible for border security and immigration services. Lawmakers need to find a solution soon to avoid a government shutdown. Some experts say this political fight shows how difficult immigration reform can be in the US.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **face uncertainty** — 불확실성에 직면하다  
>   _The project **faces uncertainty** because of lack of funds._  
> - **stop a compromise** — 타협을 막다  
>   _Disagreements can **stop a compromise** in important talks._  
> - **cause problems** — 문제를 일으키다  
>   _Delays in funding **cause problems** for many people._  
> - **find a solution** — 해결책을 찾다  
>   _We must **find a solution** to this issue quickly._

## 3. US Job Market Shows Signs of Improvement in March  
![Article 3](/images/news/2026-03-29-3.png)

After a slow start in February, the US job market seems to be **thawing out** in March. This means more people are finding jobs and companies are hiring again. Experts say the economy is recovering from a cold period when fewer jobs were available. This is good news for workers who were worried about losing their jobs. Some industries like technology and health care are growing faster than others. However, the job market can still change depending on the economy and global events. Many people hope this positive trend will continue in the coming months.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **thaw out** — 풀리다, 좋아지다 (상황, 분위기 등)  
>   _The job market is starting to **thaw out** after a slow winter._  
> - **find a job** — 일자리를 찾다  
>   _Many people are trying to **find a job** in growing industries._  
> - **recover from** — ~로부터 회복하다  
>   _The economy is beginning to **recover from** last year’s problems._  
> - **positive trend** — 긍정적인 추세  
>   _There is a **positive trend** in hiring this month._

## 4. Silicon Valley Remains a Leading Tech Hub in the US  
![Article 4](/images/news/2026-03-29-4.png)

Silicon Valley, located in California, is still known as the most important center for technology and innovation in the US. It is home to many big tech companies and startups. The area attracts smart workers and investors from around the world. Silicon Valley helps create new ideas in software, hardware, and internet services. This tech hub also faces challenges like global competition and political issues. Still, experts believe it will keep growing and influencing the world’s technology. For many, Silicon Valley is a symbol of creativity and progress in the digital age.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **tech hub** — 기술 중심지  
>   _Silicon Valley is a famous **tech hub** in the United States._  
> - **innovation** — 혁신

> - **attract investors** — 투자자를 끌어들이다

> - **face challenges** — 어려움에 직면하다
