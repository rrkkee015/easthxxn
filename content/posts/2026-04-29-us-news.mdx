## 1. Federal Courts Reject Trump’s Immigration Detention Policy ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-29-1.png)

The US appeals courts have recently **ruled against** the Trump administration’s new immigration detention policy. This policy would have required mandatory detention for many immigrants, even those without criminal records. The courts said the policy was too harsh and did not follow the law properly. This decision could **sharply reduce** how many immigrants are held in detention centers. It also affects the overall population growth in the US because immigration plays a big role. The ruling shows the courts are willing to **push back** on strict immigration rules. We may see more legal challenges as immigration remains a hot topic in America.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **rule against** — ~에 반대 판결을 내리다  
>   _The court **ruled against** the new policy last week._  
> - **sharply reduce** — 급격히 줄이다  
>   _The new law could **sharply reduce** the number of people detained._  
> - **push back** — 반발하다, 거부하다  
>   _Citizens **pushed back** against the strict rules._

## 2. US Jobs Report Shows Hiring Growth but Future Uncertain ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-29-2.png)

The US government reported that 178,000 workers were hired in March. This is a sign that the job market is still growing, but the pace has slowed down compared to earlier months. Experts say the **hiring boomlet** might not last long because the economy faces challenges like inflation and global issues. Many companies are still looking for skilled workers, especially in technology and healthcare. The unemployment rate remains low, which means most people who want jobs can find them. However, some workers worry about job security and pay raises in the future. The report helps people understand the current **job market** and what might come next.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **hiring boomlet** — 작은 고용 붐, 고용 증가의 작은 흐름  
>   _There was a **hiring boomlet** in the technology sector last quarter._  
> - **job market** — 취업 시장  
>   _The **job market** has become more competitive this year._  
> - **pay raise** — 임금 인상  
>   _Many employees are asking for a **pay raise** due to inflation._

## 3. Silicon Valley’s Shift Toward Conservatism ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-29-3.png)

Silicon Valley, known for its tech innovations, is seeing a political change. More people in the tech industry are now identifying as conservatives or leaning right. This shift is surprising because the area has been mostly liberal for years. Some experts say this change is because of concerns about free speech, government rules, and the economy. The rise of the “Silicon Valley Right” could **reshape the tech landscape** and affect how companies behave. It might change hiring practices, product development, and public statements. Watching this trend is important because Silicon Valley influences global technology and culture.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **reshape the tech landscape** — 기술 분야의 모습을 바꾸다  
>   _New policies could **reshape the tech landscape** in the next decade._  
> - **rise of** — ~의 증가, 부상  
>   _The **rise of** remote work changed many industries._  
> - **leaning right** — 보수 쪽으로 기울다  
>   _More employees are **leaning right** politically._

## 4. FCC Orders Early License Renewal for ABC Stations After Controversy ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-29-4.png)

The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has ordered ABC television stations to renew their licenses early. This action came after a joke about the first lady made by late-night host Jimmy Kimmel caused public complaints. The FCC wants to make sure the stations follow broadcasting rules carefully. The decision shows the government is watching TV content more closely than before. This case may affect how TV networks handle sensitive topics and humor in the future. It also raises questions about freedom of speech and regulation on public media.

> ### 영어 표현 학습  
> - **license renewal** — 면허 갱신  
>   _The station applied for an early **license renewal**._  
> - **public complaints** — 대중의 불만  
>   _There were many **public complaints** about the controversial show._  
> - **broadcasting rules** — 방송 규칙  
>   _All networks must follow strict **broadcasting rules**._
