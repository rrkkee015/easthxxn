## 1. Trump Advises US Negotiators to Take Time on Iran Deal 

Former President Donald Trump told American negotiators **not to rush** into a new deal with Iran. The talks aim to bring Iran back into an agreement limiting its nuclear program. Trump’s warning means the US team should be careful and think carefully before making a deal. The negotiations are important because they affect peace and security in the Middle East and beyond. Experts say a rushed deal might miss important details or give too much to Iran. So, the US government wants to balance quick progress with safety. The next steps will show if both sides can agree without pressure.

## 2. New Rule: Foreigners Must Apply for Green Cards Outside the US 

The Trump administration created a new rule that foreigners must apply for green cards outside the United States. This means people cannot get their green card while staying in the US. They have to leave the country and apply from their home country or another place. The government says this rule will make the process clearer and stop some delays. However, many immigrants worry this will make it harder to stay in the US legally. It could also affect families who live apart during the application. The rule changes how immigration works and might cause problems for many hopeful immigrants.

> - **outside the US** — 미국 밖에서  
>   _Due to the new rule, you must apply **outside the US**._

> - **stay legally** — 합법적으로 머무르다

## 3. International Students Face Growing Barriers in US Job Market 

International students in the US are finding it harder to get jobs after graduation. Many say the **American dream is collapsing** because new rules and policies create obstacles. Employers sometimes prefer hiring US citizens, and visa rules are stricter. This makes it difficult for students to stay and work legally. Experts warn this could hurt the US economy because these students often have important skills. Students feel frustrated and worried about their futures. The government and companies may need to find solutions to keep attracting global talent.

## 4. Silicon Valley Pressure Blocks Trump’s AI Order 

Silicon Valley companies used their influence to stop a Trump administration order on artificial intelligence (AI). The order was expected to regulate AI technology quickly, but tech leaders asked for more time. They want to **rein in AI** carefully to avoid mistakes or harm. Many experts agree that AI should be controlled but also supported for growth. The debate shows the power of Silicon Valley in shaping technology rules. The US government now faces pressure to find a balanced way to handle AI’s future.

> - **take more time** — 더 시간을 가지다
