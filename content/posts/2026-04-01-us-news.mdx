## 1. Judge Stops Trump’s White House Ballroom Project ![Article 1](/images/news/2026-04-01-1.png)

A judge has recently **halted** the construction of a new ballroom at the White House that was planned by former President Trump. The project had been moving forward but faced legal challenges due to concerns about costs and historic preservation rules. This decision means the construction work must stop until the legal issues are resolved. The ballroom was meant to be a large space for events and gatherings, but critics say it could be too expensive and unnecessary. Supporters argue it would modernize the White House facilities. This case shows how important legal rules can **impact** big government projects. It also reflects ongoing debates about how public money should be spent.

## 2. New Immigration Policies Affect Immigrant Children’s Education ![Article 2](/images/news/2026-04-01-2.png)

Recent federal and state policies are making it harder for immigrant children to get equal access to education. These changes could **erode** many years of progress that helped immigrant children feel welcome and supported in schools. Some policies focus on stricter rules about language use and documentation. Experts worry these rules will cause more children to miss out on important learning opportunities. Education access is critical because it helps children succeed in the future. Many groups are calling for better support and less strict policies to protect immigrant students. This situation shows how laws outside of schools can still strongly **impact** education.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **erode** — 약화시키다, 침식하다  
>   _The constant rain will **erode** the soil over time._  
> - **access** — 접근, 이용할 기회  
>   _Everyone should have **access** to quality education._  
> - **policy** — 정책  
>   _The government introduced a new **policy** on immigration._  
> - **impact** — 영향을 주다  
>   _The new law will **impact** many families._

## 3. US Job Openings Fall to Lowest Since Pandemic ![Article 3](/images/news/2026-04-01-3.png)

In February, the number of available jobs in the United States dropped to the lowest level since the COVID-19 pandemic began. This shows that companies are **hiring** fewer workers than before. Experts say this might mean the job market is slowing down or becoming less active. Some reasons for this include economic uncertainty and higher costs for businesses. The slowdown could make it harder for some people to find new jobs. However, it does not mean all jobs are lost; some industries still need workers. The job market’s changes are important because they affect how people plan their careers and finances.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **job market** — 구직 시장  
>   _The **job market** can be very competitive in big cities._  
> - **hiring** — 고용하는 것  
>   _The company is **hiring** new employees this month._  
> - **slow down** — 느려지다, 둔화되다  
>   _Sales **slowed down** during the winter season._  
> - **economic uncertainty** — 경제적 불확실성  
>   _**Economic uncertainty** makes businesses cautious._

## 4. Silicon Valley and Washington Need to Work Together, Says Expert ![Article 4](/images/news/2026-04-01-4.png)

Trae Stephens, a well-known investor, says Silicon Valley and Washington must **build together** to solve big problems. He means tech companies and the government should cooperate more. Right now, the two often have different goals and ideas. Stephens believes that by working closely, they can create better rules and innovations. This could help the US stay strong in technology and security. The idea is important because technology affects many parts of life and the economy. Building a stronger partnership could bring many benefits for the future.

> ### 영어 표현 학습
> - **build together** — 함께 협력하다, 같이 만들다  
>   _Communities can **build together** to improve local schools._  
> - **cooperate** — 협력하다  
>   _The two companies decided to **cooperate** on the new project._  
> - **innovation** — 혁신, 새로움  
>   _Technology companies focus on **innovation** to stay competitive._  
> - **partnership** — 협력 관계, 동반자 관계  
>   _The school formed a **partnership** with local businesses._
